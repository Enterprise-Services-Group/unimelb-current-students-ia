Student services — fragmentation deep-dive

How each current-student service is scattered across the 20-domain estate, and the consolidation map. June 2026.
University of Melbourne  ·  June 2026

How each current-student service is spread across the University's 20-domain public web estate, traced through the path-level link graph (21,250 pages). One profile per service: its true footprint, canonical owner, the seams a student hits, the duplication, and the consolidation action. June 2026.

Executive summary

Read service-by-service, the estate's fragmentation has a consistent shape. For most services the central hub students.unimelb.edu.au is the right owner and is reached — the problem is rarely "no front door." The problem is what happens downstream of the front door: the hub duplicates itself across parallel URL trees, the actual service delivery sits on a different host (often branded as something else, or behind login, or off the crawled estate entirely), and every faculty re-hosts its own copy rather than linking the centre. A student looking for one service routes through three or four hosts with inconsistent labels to reach the thing they need — and sometimes the most authoritative version is a draft, a deprecated tree, or a prospective-marketing page.

Six structural patterns recur across the services below. They are not fourteen separate problems; they are six systemic faults expressed fourteen times — which is what makes them fixable as patterns, not page-by-page.

The six recurring fragmentation patterns

1. The hub duplicates itself — parallel URL trees mid-migration. Almost every service that the hub owns is served under two or three live URL roots at once — /support-and-wellbeing vs legacy /student-support (wellbeing, equity, international, contacts), /course-admin vs /your-course vs /admin (fees, enrolment, graduation, exams). The legacy tree usually carries rel=canonical to the new one yet stays live and internally linked, so the same content competes with itself for inbound links and search rank. Affects: wellbeing, equity, fees, international, enrolment, graduation, exams, contacts.

2. The canonical owner exists but is barely reached — or doesn't exist at all. Scholarships has a dedicated 2,124-page scholarships.unimelb but the hub links it ~37×; Student IT's real home studentit.unimelb and HDR's gradresearch.unimelb sit outside the student estate entirely; placements/WIL has no central owner at all (zero WIL front door on the hub). The owner is an afterthought, off-estate, or absent. Affects: scholarships, IT, HDR, careers, placements.

3. The action lives behind login; the public estate only describes it. The thing a student actually does — pay fees, enrol in a subject, book a counselling appointment, apply to graduate, log an IT ticket, RSVP a careers event — happens in my.unimelb, Canvas, ServiceNow or a *.app form, all behind auth and uncrawlable. The public pages narrate the service and link to a login wall, so the find→act seam is invisible from the outside and unverifiable. Affects: IT, careers, enrolment, graduation, fees, wellbeing.

4. Delivery sits on a shared or off-brand host. Counselling and the Health Service live on services.unimelb, a multi-service host that is described as "Academic Skills Unit" (614×) and "DELA" (613×) far more than "Counselling" (39×) — so a wellbeing link lands on a page branded as something else. Safer Community and campus safety are separate sites again. The student crosses a host boundary onto an unfamiliar brand mid-task. Affects: wellbeing, academic skills, safety.

5. The reverse-funnel — prospective owns the content the enrolled student needs. Fees content is 363 pages on the prospective study.unimelb vs ~17 on the hub; careers' single biggest owner is study.unimelb (440 pages); scholarships' discovery engine is prospective-facing. Enrolled students are bounced back up the funnel to marketing pages for answers they need now. Affects: fees, careers, scholarships.

6. Faculty re-invention and byte-identical clones. Faculties re-host the central service rather than link it — and often verbatim: 5 Jaccard-1.0 wellbeing clones and the entire HDR candidature lifecycle duplicated byte-for-byte across Education and MDHS; the BCom careers tree double-hosted by FBE and MBS; placements scattered across 8 faculty silos under 4 different names; student-life across 12+ faculty silos. The same content is maintained N times and drifts out of sync. Affects: HDR, wellbeing, placements, student-life, careers, equity.

Per-service deep-dives

wellbeing, health & safety [HIGH]

The central front door exists and is reached (students.unimelb/support-and-wellbeing), but the service is shattered downstream: two parallel trees on the hub itself, the actual counselling/health services live on a separate shared host, and safer-community/safety sit on their own uncrawled domains — so a student routes through 3-4 hosts with inconsistent labels to reach help.

Spread: 19 domains · 115 URL trees · central-hub pages 170 · top owner medicine (1073). True footprint. Raw classifier tagged 2,588 pages across 19 domains; filtered TRUE current-student footprint is ~335 rows / ~244 unique query-stripped paths. Filtered OUT: 1,488 medical-school false positives (medicine.unimelb 1,073 = /school-structure/medical-education, surgery, paediatrics, research-groups; mdhs 211 + biomedicalsciences 204 = research/org/news/dept pages, keyword 'health'), and 428 study.unimelb prospective pages (379 = /find/courses catalogue). True footprint = hub 167 (students.unimelb /support-and-wellbeing + legacy /student-support) + delivery 127 (services.unimelb /counsel ~100, /health ~20) + ~41 genuine per-faculty current-student wellbeing stubs (education, law, science, fbe, eng, biomed graduate-research). Canonical owner. Canonical owner is students.unimelb.edu.au — and unlike most services it IS reached. The genuine front door /support-and-wellbeing (title 'Support and wellbeing | Current students') fans out to all the real providers. But the hub only ROUTES; it owns none of the actual service delivery, which lives on services.unimelb (counsel/health) and on separate uncrawled domains (safercommunity.unimelb, safety.unimelb) plus the authenticated core (my.unimelb appointments). Ownership is split front-door-vs-delivery, and even the front door is duplicated under two URL roots.

The seams a student hits

  • Front-door duplication: the hub serves the SAME service under two roots — /support-and-wellbeing (live) and /student-support (legacy, rel=canonical but still crawlable and linked) — sharing 6 identical leaf paths.
  • Front-door-to-delivery hop crosses hosts: the advice ('get counselling') lives on students.unimelb but the service lives on services.unimelb/counsel — a domain switch (290 hub->services contextual links) onto a host that mixes 5 unrelated services.
  • Label inconsistency at the delivery host: services.unimelb is described as 'Academic Skills Unit' (614), 'DELA' (613), 'Student Financial Aid' (125) FAR more than 'Counselling and Psychological Services' (39+21) or 'Health Service' (18) — a student following a wellbeing link lands on a host branded as something else.
  • Safety split onto an off-estate domain: the Safer Community Program is a separate site (safercommunity.unimelb, 62 hub links) with inconsistent anchors ('Safer Community Program', 'Speak Safely Portal', 'The Safer Community Program', 'Bullying') — the safety half of 'wellbeing, health & safety' is one extra hop away and not in the crawled estate.
  • Faculty detours: 6 faculties (education, mdhs, eng, law, science, fbe) each host their own wellbeing/student-support page rather than deep-linking the central hub, so an HDR or faculty student may never reach students.unimelb at all.
  • Dead ends in the delivery path: dead-pages.csv shows broken counsel links (services.unimelb/counsel/about/meet-our-counsellors, /counsel/resources/mental-health-problems, /counsel/404) and broken legacy hub links (/student-support/student-equity-and-disability-support/..., /support-and-wellbeing/stop-1/categories/more-services) — students hit 404s mid-help-seeking.

Duplication

  • TWO parallel wellbeing trees on the hub itself: /support-and-wellbeing (109 paths) and legacy /student-support (10 paths) share 6 identical leaf paths; legacy page is rel=canonical to the live one yet remains live and linked.
  • 5 EXACT (jaccard=1.0) clones of HDR student-support pages across education.unimelb and mdhs.unimelb: counselling, safer-community-program, housing-support, student-services-directory, library — byte-for-byte identical (1341/693/767/2073 words).
  • Per-faculty wellbeing stubs duplicating the central offer: eng /students/coursework/study-resources/wellbeing, law /students/academic-support-and-wellbeing, science /students/student-life/wellbeing, fbe /students/wellbeing, biomedicalsciences /study/current-student-information.
  • OSHC (Overseas Student Health Cover) authored repeatedly: /course-admin/study-overseas/.../health-and-wellbeing, /sandbox/2026-uplifts/student-visas/...oshc, /student-support/international-student-support/visas/...oshc, /support-and-wellbeing/international-student-support/visas/...oshc — same content under 4 path variants on the hub.
  • University Health Promotion Program tree fully duplicated under BOTH hub roots (/student-support/health-and-wellbeing/university-health-promotion-program/* and /support-and-wellbeing/health-and-wellbeing/university-health-promotion-program/*), including health-promotion-grants, safer-sex, vape-free-campus.

Consolidation action. Make students.unimelb.edu.au/support-and-wellbeing the single, enforced front door and collapse everything else into it. (1) Retire the legacy /student-support tree — 301-redirect all 10 paths to their /support-and-wellbeing equivalents (the rel=canonical already declares intent) so only one hub tree remains. (2) Re-brand or sub-path the delivery host: surface Counselling & Psychological Services and the Health Service under a wellbeing-branded entry (or move them under students.unimelb/support-and-wellbeing/...) so students don't cross onto services.unimelb where the dominant labels are Academic Skills/DELA/Financial Aid; fix the broken /counsel/* links. (3) Pull the safer-community/safety content into the hub's safety-and-security node with ONE consistent label, deep-linking safercommunity.unimelb rather than scattering 'Speak Safely Portal'/'Safer Community Program' variants. (4) Replace the 5 exact HDR clones and the 6 faculty wellbeing stubs with transcluded links to the central hub, deleting the duplicate authored copies. (5) Consolidate the 4 OSHC variants and the doubled Health Promotion Program subtree to single canonical URLs. Net: one front door, one delivery brand, one safety entry — eliminating the host-hopping and label whiplash a distressed student currently faces.

scholarships & awards [HIGH]

Scholarships is split three ways — a prospective-facing "Find a scholarship" search engine (scholarships.unimelb), a dozen faculty-authored scholarship trees, and a fractured current-students hub section — with no single owner of the current-student "manage my scholarship" experience and the catalogue barely reached from the hub.

Spread: 17 domains · 102 URL trees · central-hub pages 37 · top owner scholarships (2124). True footprint. Raw classifier: 2,798 pages across 17 domains. TRUE current-student footprint ≈ 470–490 pages. Filtering: (1) Removed faceted-search URL bloat — 2,063 of 2,798 pages (74%) are Funnelback search-result permutations (/search?collection=...api-m1-scholarships...&start_rank=N&f.Area+of+study=...&query=...); scholarships.unimelb alone is 2,124 pages of which 2,037 are search bloat, leaving only 87 real content pages (~82 /awards/ pages + home/guide/faq). (2) Removed clinical/research false-positives — medicine.unimelb's 141 hits are research-group pages, newsletters, staff bios and "award winner" lists (e.g. /school-structure/general-practice-and-primary-care/newsletter/...), NOT a student scholarship service; keyword matched stray "award"/"scholarship". Genuine remainder: scholarships.unimelb 87 + students.unimelb 37 + faculty genuine-static scholarship trees ≈ 340 (eng 83, arts 62, finearts-music 36, fbe 33, mdhs 26, law 21, biomedicalsciences 21, education 20, science 12, others) + ~3 current-relevant study.unimelb pages. Canonical owner. scholarships.unimelb.edu.au is the dedicated central site and de jure canonical owner, but it is reached as an afterthought from the current-students estate. The current-students hub (students.unimelb) sends only 28 contextual links into it, versus 411 from study.unimelb (prospective/marketing) and 325 from medicine — i.e. the prospective funnel and a faculty out-link to it 14× and 11× more than the student hub does. Worse, scholarships.unimelb is itself prospective-shaped: its home page is titled "Find a scholarship" and 96% of it is an applicant-facing faceted search, so even when reached it does not own the current-student "manage your scholarship / payment dates / conditions" job — that lives back in students.unimelb. There is no single owner of the end-to-end current-student scholarship experience.

The seams a student hits

  • Hub → canonical catalogue is a near-broken seam: students.unimelb (current-students hub) → scholarships.unimelb is only 28 contextual links, vs study.unimelb → scholarships.unimelb 411. A current student on the hub is ~15× less likely to be routed to the central catalogue than a prospective applicant on study.unimelb.
  • Catalogue → hub return path is thin: scholarships.unimelb → students.unimelb only 16 links. Once a student lands in the applicant-shaped search, almost nothing routes them back to the current-student 'manage your scholarship' pages where their actual job lives.
  • Catalogue → transactional core is effectively absent: scholarships.unimelb → sis.unimelb 1, → forms.your.unimelb 4, → services.unimelb 5. The site that lists awards barely connects to where you accept/manage one (my.unimelb/SIS behind login).
  • Faculty → central is one-way and uneven: faculties link INTO scholarships.unimelb (medicine 325, finearts-music 221, education 150, eng 129) but the central site links back to faculties almost never (arts 1, mdhs 1) — students get funnelled up but not laterally across the discipline trees.
  • HDR scholarships straddle two owners: RTP/MRS appear both on scholarships.unimelb/awards and on gradresearch.unimelb, with faculties linking to gradresearch far more than to scholarships.unimelb — an HDR student must reconcile two central sources.
  • Dead/login-walled links inside faculty scholarship trees: arts.unimelb /scholarships/graduate-research and /scholarships/school/* return 404 (4 dead pages), eng has 2 dead Johansen-scholarship URLs, and finearts-music's Majlis Travelling Scholarship page is an orphan behind Sign-In.

Duplication

  • Catalogue duplicated across estate: ~82 central /awards/ pages are paralleled by ~340 faculty-authored scholarship/prize pages — the same awards re-listed per discipline (eng 83, arts 62, finearts-music 36, fbe 33, etc.) with no canonical cross-reference.
  • The faceted SEARCH itself is duplicated, not shared: the Funnelback collection 'api-m1-scholarships' is re-embedded as separate search-result URL spaces on scholarships.unimelb (2,037), msd (13), law (8) and mbs (6) — 2,063 near-identical search-permutation pages estate-wide instead of one canonical search.
  • Current-student 'manage your scholarship' content forked into 3+ parallel URL trees inside students.unimelb (/support-and-wellbeing/manage-your-scholarship, /student-support/scholarships-and-financial-support/manage-your-scholarship, /your-course|/course-admin/...evidence-of-scholarship) — same payment-dates / conditions content at multiple live paths from an unfinished IA migration.
  • HDR scholarship pages (RTP, Melbourne Research Scholarship) duplicated between scholarships.unimelb/awards and gradresearch.unimelb.
  • Anchor-text inconsistency for the SAME destination: scholarships.unimelb is labelled 'Search for scholarships' (111), 'Find a scholarship' (19+22), 'Scholarships' (19), 'Find out' (16), 'Discover more about Melbourne' (22), 'Contact us' (15) — no consistent name, so the canonical owner has no stable identity in navigation.

Consolidation action. Split the service cleanly into DISCOVER vs MANAGE and give each one owner. (1) Make scholarships.unimelb the single canonical DISCOVER catalogue: collapse the 2,063 faceted-search permutations to one canonical /search (canonicalise/noindex the start_rank & facet URLs), retire the msd/law/mbs duplicate search instances and point them at the central index, and convert faculty scholarship trees from re-authored listings into thin discipline-filtered views of the central catalogue. (2) Make students.unimelb the single canonical MANAGE owner: consolidate the 3 parallel 'manage-your-scholarship' path trees into one, and raise the hub→catalogue link density from 28 toward parity with study.unimelb's 411 (add 'Find a scholarship' entry points wherever a current student lands). (3) Wire the catalogue to the transactional core — every /awards page should deep-link to the my.unimelb/SIS accept-and-manage flow. (4) Standardise the anchor label to 'Find a scholarship' everywhere. (5) Fix the dead arts/eng scholarship URLs and the login-walled finearts orphan. This removes ~2,000 bloat pages, halves the genuine page count via faculty de-duplication, and gives a student one place to find an award and one place to manage it.
