# Topic Deep-Dive: Scholarships

*Cross-faculty analysis from the full crawl. June 2026.*

**141 scholarships-tagged pages** sit across the faculty estate — a mid-sized topic, but a revealing one. Unlike course planning (degree-specific by nature), most of this content is **generically structured** — application rounds, terms & conditions, FAQs, recipient honour-rolls, Dean's Honours Lists — and yet **students.unimelb.edu.au holds none of it in the crawl**. The pattern is heavily faculty-owned by *default rather than design*, dominated by a single faculty (FEIT alone is 50%), and shot through with the kind of process scaffolding (rounds, eligibility, T&Cs, winners' galleries) that is the textbook profile of a consolidation candidate.

| Engineering & IT (FEIT) | 71 | Named scholarships, application rounds, T&Cs, Dean's Honours Lists, Community Awards, recipient galleries |

One faculty — FEIT — holds **71 of 141 pages (50%)**. This is not because engineering has more scholarships than the rest of the university; it is because FEIT has built a full sub-site (`/scholarships-prizes-and-awards/…`) with a page for every round, every named award, every year's Dean's Honours List and Community Awards gallery — much of it duplicated across two URL trees (the old `/coursework/orientation/…` path and the new `/scholarships-prizes-and-awards/…` path).

## Types of Scholarships content

1. **Named donor / sponsored scholarship pages** — one page per individual award. *FEIT* is saturated with these: "Lens Stevens Scholarship", "Ian Alexander Scholarship Round 2", "John Balfour Memorial Scholarship", "Stephen Ho Accelerator Grant", "Acusensus Scholarship for Women in STEM". *Law* GRD adds "James and Valerie McNiff Scholarship". This is the genuinely faculty-specific layer — the awards exist because a faculty/donor created them.
2. **Application-round / cycle pages** — the same scholarship process sliced by intake. *FEIT* "Round 1 / Round 2 / Round 3 / Round 4 Scholarships", "Summer Round, 2026", "Winter Round, 2026". A scheduling artefact: each round is a near-identical page that differs mainly in dates, and several are re-hosted under the events calendar as well.
3. **Terms & conditions / eligibility / FAQ scaffolding** — the rulebook around the money. *FEIT* "Engineering & IT Coursework Scholarship Terms and Conditions" (present twice, on two paths), "Eligibility and selection criteria", "Scholarship FAQ", "Frequently Asked Questions"; *Science* "Frequently asked questions about scholarships" (1,962w — the single longest page on the topic). This is generic process content, not discipline knowledge.
4. **Scholarship catalogues / landing pages** — the "here's what's available" index. *Science* "Awards, prizes and scholarships", "Scholarships", "Open scholarships", "Industry, external and other research scholarships"; *FEIT* "Scholarships, Prizes and Awards", "Undergraduate Students", "Coursework Students", "Graduate Researchers"; *Education* "Faculty of Education Scholarships". Several Arts entries point directly at the **central `scholarships.unimelb.edu.au`** catalogue ("Faculty of Arts Internship Grants", "Conference Grants") — evidence a central award system already exists but isn't surfaced as students.unimelb.edu.au's own.
5. **Dean's Honours Lists & academic-merit recognition** — annual high-achiever lists. *FEIT* "2022 / 2023 / 2024 Dean's Honours List" + "Honour roll" + "Eligibility and selection criteria"; *Science* "Dean's Honours List", "Dean's Honours List 2023", "2023 Medal Winners", "2024 Medal Winners". A recurring, near-templated content type that accretes one new page per year.
6. **Recipient profiles & winners' galleries** — recognition/marketing content. *FEIT* "Scholarships, Prizes and Awards Recipients", "Community Awards" + "2022–2025 winners" + four years of "Spotlight Celebration Image Gallery"; *MDHS* "Chancellor's Scholars" profiles (Garry Zhu, Emily Sun, Zahra Ataie); *Law* GRD researcher profiles (Renuka Balasubramaniam, Janelle Koh, etc.). More celebration than transaction — arguably not "current student services" at all.
7. **Prizes & awards (distinct from scholarships)** — merit prizes rather than funding. *FEIT* "Coursework Prizes", "Undergraduate Prizes", "Graduate Research Prizes", "Stephen Ho Innovation Awards"; *Science* "Prizes for top performing students"; *FBE* "Graduate researcher prizes and awards"; *Law* "Student Published Research Prize". Frequently bundled into the same "Scholarships, Prizes and Awards" trees.
8. **Travel, conference, mobility & internship grants** — funding tied to an activity. *Arts* "Faculty of Arts Travel Grant", "Conference Grants", "Internship Grants", "Melbourne Mobility Awards", "Arts Global Languages Scholarship"; *FEIT* "Conference Travel Grant", "Student Exchange Funding and Scholarships"; *FFAM* "Grants and internal funding". Overlaps heavily with the *overseas/exchange* and *research-candidature* topics.
9. **Graduate-researcher (HDR) scholarships & funding** — stipends, fee remissions, write-up awards. *FBE* "FBE Doctoral Program Stipend and Tuition (Fee Remission) Scholarships", "Stipends and fee remissions", "Research training and development scholarships"; *FFAM* "Scholarships & Studentships", "MCM Director's Writing Up Award"; *Science* "Graduate research scholarships"; *Dental* "Melbourne Dental School Internal Grants"; *FEIT* "PhD Write Up Award". A distinct audience (HDR) whose funding is partly governed centrally by the Graduate Research school.

A cross-cutting observation: at least **a third of the FEIT corpus is duplication or churn** — the same scholarship/T&C/round page reachable from two URL trees, plus one new page per year for each Honours List and Spotlight gallery. The topic's page-count is inflated by structure, not substance.

## Legitimate vs central-overlapping

| Faculty-owned (cannot be centralised) | Overlaps students.unimelb.edu.au (consolidation candidate) |
|---|---|
| Named donor/sponsored awards specific to a faculty (FEIT, Law GRD) | Generic scholarship FAQs, eligibility and "how to apply" process content |
| Discipline-specific HDR stipends & internal research grants (FBE, FFAM, Dental) | Application-round pages (Round 1–4, Summer/Winter) — date-driven, near-identical |
| Faculty-specific prizes tied to named subjects/competitions | Terms & conditions templates (FEIT carries the same T&C on two paths) |
| Activity grants bound to faculty programs (Arts mobility, FFAM funding) | Dean's Honours Lists & recipient/winner galleries (recognition, not service) |
| | Faculty catalogues that merely re-list awards already in `scholarships.unimelb.edu.au` |

The balance here is the **inverse of course planning**. There, most content was legitimately degree-specific. Here, the genuinely faculty-owned layer is thin (the specific *awards* and HDR funding), while a large majority of pages — rounds, T&Cs, FAQs, Honours Lists, recipient galleries, catalogue restatements — is generic process and recognition content that a central scholarships system could own or template.

## Recommendation

- **students.unimelb.edu.au owns the spine:** the University already runs `scholarships.unimelb.edu.au` (Arts links straight into it). Promote it to the canonical, centrally-surfaced catalogue + application engine — one place for eligibility, application rounds, T&Cs and FAQs, parameterised per award. Faculties stop hand-maintaining round/T&C/FAQ pages.
- **Faculties keep the awards, not the plumbing:** each faculty retains a thin page describing *its* named/donor scholarships and discipline-specific HDR funding, deep-linking to the central system for the apply-and-rules mechanics.
- **Add one students.unimelb.edu.au gateway:** `students.unimelb.edu.au/your-finances/scholarships` (or under support/finance) — a single entry point listing every faculty's awards and the central catalogue. students.unimelb.edu.au currently holds **zero** scholarship pages, so students have no central starting point at all.
- **Set a recognition standard:** Dean's Honours Lists, winners' galleries and recipient profiles are marketing/recognition, not current-student services — move them out of the services IA (to faculty news/alumni) or template them so they stop accreting one new orphan page per year.
- **Highest-value fix:** de-duplicate FEIT. Collapsing the two parallel URL trees and the year-by-year Honours/Spotlight churn would remove roughly a third of this topic's pages and fix its single worst maintenance liability — before any cross-faculty consolidation even begins.
