🎯 Five Root Findings

1. The estate describes; it never transacts

Every student journey terminates in a walled system. The public estate is an instruction manual for systems it cannot show.

2. The hub competes with itself

833 pages mid-migration, 120 leaf pages dual-served under two parallel URL trees. 12 domains link the deprecated trees.

3. The joints are broken — and the cheapest to fix

Graduate→alumni: 35 pages, 0 links. Apply→enrol: 373 links. These are purely additive fixes — nothing to unpick.

4. Fragmentation is regressive

The students who can least afford to hunt across 5-8 systems are the ones who must. Indigenous support written at graduate reading level.

5. The fix is structural, not visual

A thin student overlay on Squiz, a subdomain registry, and governance — not 12 separate redesigns.
