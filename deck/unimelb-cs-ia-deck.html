Findings

Three Structural Problems

🔴 Careers is the most duplicated service

8/9 faculties + MBS run parallel career content. No cross-linking to the central careers hub.

🟡 School-level fragmentation

Biomedical Sciences, Dental School, and MBS run separate CS sections on their own subdomains — breaking the faculty model.

🔵 No "where to go" guidance

No page tells students which system handles which need. Discovery is trial and error.

🔵 Hub has genuine gaps

Zero content for placements/WIL, course plans, discipline-specific academic skills, or program regulatory requirements.

Recommendation

Hub-and-Spoke Model

Hub Owns

  • Enrolment & course admin
  • Fees & key dates
  • Exams, results, graduation
  • Special consideration
  • Health & wellbeing
  • Careers platform & tools
  • Student life framework
  • Study overseas

Both Provide

  • Course planning (hub: general; faculty: degree-specific)
  • Careers (hub: platform; faculty: discipline mentoring)

Faculty Owns

  • Sample course plans & degree guides
  • Placement/practicum management
  • Faculty-specific forms
  • Discipline-specific academic skills
  • Program regulatory requirements
  • Faculty scholarships & prizes
  • Faculty clubs, events, newsletters
  • Performance/practice facilities (FFAM)

Governance

6 Design Principles

1. Students should never have to know which system handles what. The hub is always the first stop.
2. One source of truth per service. No duplication between faculty and hub.
3. Consistent URL conventions: faculty.unimelb.edu.au/students for every faculty.
4. Standard navigation label: "Current students" — not "Students" / "Study" / "Student resources".
5. Faculty pages link out; hub pages link down. Always explicit about where to go next.
6. "Where to go" is always explicit. Never make students infer the correct system.
The goal is not to centralise everything.
The goal is that students never notice there are multiple systems.
