## What this means

### 1. Student IT is well-connected to the hub

34 links to the student hub in 30 sampled pages — stronger than the library (36) per-page. The hub→studentit connection (55 links) is also healthy. This is a working bidirectional seam.

### 2. The offboarding section serves graduating students

13 pages on what to do before leaving — download data, migrate accounts, preserve access. This is the IT side of the graduation→alumni transition. The graduation deep-dive should link here.

### 3. Practical, task-focused content

Unlike the marketing-heavy online.unimelb or the club-focused umsu, studentit is purely operational: "how do I connect to wifi," "how do I print," "how do I download my data before I leave." This is exactly the kind of content the hub should route students to.

1. **Add Student IT to the hub's "IT & systems" section prominently.** A student troubleshooting wifi or printing should find studentit from the hub.

2. **Link the graduation offboarding pages from the graduation→alumni bridge.** The "download your data before you leave" pages are the IT side of the graduation transition.

3. **No urgent issues.** Well-connected, well-maintained, task-focused.
