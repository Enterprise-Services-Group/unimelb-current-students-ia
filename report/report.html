Executive Summary

The University of Melbourne runs a fragmented current-students ecosystem: 9 faculty sites plus the central hub (students.unimelb.edu.au), totalling approximately 780 pages across 650,000 words. The fragmentation is not uniform — four faculties (Law, FEIT, Arts, Science) operate full parallel ecosystems while others (ABP, FBE) function as link directories to the central hub.

Core Problem

Students cannot reliably find information because the same concept ("current students") lives at four different URL patterns (/students, /current-students, /study/current-students, /study/student-resources), across 9+ subdomains, with no consistent guidance on which system handles which need.

Recommendation

Adopt a hub-and-spoke model with clear service boundaries. The central hub becomes the definitive source for all transactional and cross-cutting services. Faculties retain genuinely discipline-specific content (course plans, placements, forms, program requirements) but adopt a standardised template and consistent URL convention.

Structural Findings

Finding 1: The single concept "current students" is served at four different URL patterns with no discoverability by convention.
Finding 2: Faculties fall on a spectrum from "hub-dependent link directory" to "self-contained parallel ecosystem." FEIT (204 pages, 174 unique, 0 link-farm) is the most self-contained; ABP and FBE are mostly link directories.
Finding 3 — School-level fragmentation: Three MDHS schools (Biomedical Sciences, Dental, MSPGH) and MBS run their own CS sections on separate subdomains. A Biomedicine student navigates 3+ different CS experiences.
Finding 4: Careers & employability is the most duplicated service. All 9 faculties and MBS run parallel career content with no cross-linking to the central hub.
Finding 5: Course planning duplication is partially legitimate — every faculty must provide degree-specific course plans — but general "how to plan" advice is duplicated from the hub unnecessarily.
Finding 6: Broken and circular links exist across the ecosystem: FBE /current-students → 404, FEIT has 17 broken/duplicate URLs, Arts has 3 broken pages, MDHS school resource links are circular.
Finding 7: The central hub has no content for placements/WIL, course plans, discipline-specific academic skills, or program regulatory requirements — all of which exist only on faculty sites.
Finding 8: No faculty CS page tells students "for X, go to the hub; for Y, stay here." Students discover the correct system through trial and error.
Finding 9: CMS/platform inconsistency — three different visual templates used across faculties (Squiz Matrix for Law/Arts/FEIT/Science, card-grid for MDHS, thin-menu for FFAM).
Finding 10: Law and FEIT together account for 51% of all CS pages (399 of ~780). The four fully-crawled faculties account for 75%.

Design Principles & Governance

6 Design Principles

  1. Students should never have to know which system handles what. The hub is always the first stop. If the hub doesn't handle it, the hub links to the faculty page that does.
  2. One source of truth per service. No service should be explained on both a faculty page and the hub.
  3. Consistent URL conventions. faculty.unimelb.edu.au/students for every faculty. Redirect old patterns.
  4. Standard navigation labels. "Current students" — not "Students" / "Student resources" / "Current student information" / "Study".
  5. Faculty pages link out; hub pages link down. Faculty → hub for transactional. Hub → faculty for discipline-specific.
  6. "Where to go" is always explicit. Never make students infer the correct system.

The Decision: Four Options

Each option represents a different trade-off between centralisation, faculty autonomy, implementation speed, and technical complexity. None are binary — each can be tuned.

Option A: Full Centralisation (3 variants)

VariantModelPages on hubTimelineFaculty control?
A1: Full Consolidation Move all ~600 faculty CS pages to hub under /your-faculty/<slug>/ ~6006–12 moNo
A2: Harmonise-then-centralise First remove ~200 duplicative pages, then migrate remaining ~400 unique pages ~40012–18 moNo
A3: CDP Personalisation Harmonise first (same as A2 Step 1), then use Optimizely + Tealium CDP to personalise the hub — surfacing faculty-specific links based on known student attributes. Content stays on faculty sites. ~2012–18 moYes
When to choose A: Single namespace is the overriding priority. All three variants use distributed authorship — faculties manage their own pages on the hub via RACI and CMS permissions. A3 additionally requires Optimizely + Tealium CDP in the martech stack.

Option B: Federated Standards

Keep the current model but enforce URL conventions, template standards, cross-linking, and "where to go" guidance through governance. 3–6 months. Low cost. No content moves. Doesn't eliminate duplication — just signposts it.

Option C: Hub-and-Spoke RECOMMENDED

Hub owns transactional + cross-cutting. Faculties own discipline-specific under standardised template. ~14 new hub pages. ~200 faculty pages removed. 12 months in 3 phases. Content split by service type, not organizational unit.

Option D: Status Quo + Minimal Fixes

Fix broken links, add redirects, voluntarily adopt "where to go" sidebars. 1–2 months. Very low cost. No structural change. Duplication persists.

Why Hub-and-Spoke?

  1. Matches the evidence. Crawl data shows ~200 pages are genuine duplication (can be removed), ~400-450 are genuinely discipline-specific (should stay). Hub-and-spoke maps directly to this split.
  2. Realistic. Works with existing CMS infrastructure. No content migration. No API federation across 3+ platforms.
  3. Preserves investment. Law, FEIT, Arts, and Science have built genuinely useful faculty content over years. This preserves it while removing confusion.

Five Fixes Regardless of Choice

Standardise URLs. faculty.unimelb.edu.au/students. Redirect old patterns.
"Where do I go for X?" sidebar on every faculty CS page.
Consolidate school-level CS. Biomedical, Dental, MBS → same template as faculties.
Fix broken links. FEIT (17), Arts (3), FBE 404, MDHS circular.
Cross-link careers. Mandatory bidirectional links between hub and every faculty.
