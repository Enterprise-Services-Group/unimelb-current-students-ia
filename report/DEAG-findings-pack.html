Decision at a glance

A full crawl of the current-students estate — 1,173 pages across the central hub, 9 faculty sections and 3 school sections — finds the estate is 85% unique, discipline-specific content, not duplication. The service model is structurally sound but the seams are broken: six URL conventions, careers siloed across 9 faculties with zero cross-linking, the hub missing services students need (placements, course planning), school sites splintering the experience, and ~50 broken pages.

DEAG is asked to endorse three things:

  1. Direction — a hub-and-spoke model (hub owns the transactional spine + fills its gaps; faculties keep their discipline estates under shared standards). Not full centralisation; not status quo.
  2. No-regrets actions now — standard URL, a "where do I go for X" component, fix the broken links, connect careers. No structural decision needed.
  3. A standards mandate + a scoped Phase 2 — so the seams get fixed and stay fixed.

01  Purpose & scope

The student web experience is delivered jointly by the central hub (students.unimelb.edu.au) and current-students sections run independently by each faculty — and, in some cases, individual schools. DEAG asked whether that service model is still working, where content overlaps or has gaps, and what should change.

This pack answers from direct evidence: every reachable current-students page across the estate was crawled, classified and topic-tagged.

In scope: the central hub + all 9 faculty current-students sections + the 3 schools that run their own (Melbourne Business School, Biomedical Sciences, Melbourne Dental School). Out of scope: authenticated systems (my.unimelb, Canvas/LMS, SIS), prospective-student and staff content.

02  Method (and its limits)

A browser-based crawler walked each section breadth-first, capturing for every page its content, word count, in-section structure, outbound links (where it sends students), a unique/duplicative classification, and topic tags. 1,173 pages were captured.

Limitations, stated honestly. ABP/MSD hit a 250-page ceiling owing to a large self-referencing studio archive (~700 further pages of one repeating type — counted as one content class, not omitted findings). The hub was mapped at IA + section-landing level, not leaf-by-leaf. Classification is automated (indicative, not hand-audited). Cloudflare actively blocked conventional crawling — itself a finding for any future automated audit.

03  What we found

1  The estate is large, and overwhelmingly unique

997 of 1,173 pages (85%) are unique, substantive content. Only ~4% are pure link-farms and ~7% mixed. The dominant content is exactly what cannot live centrally: subjects & timetabling (324 pages), research candidature (243), forms & admin (213), course planning (208), placements & WIL (207). This is the most important finding — the faculty estate is real content, not redundant signposting. "Just centralise it" is not a credible option.

2  Every faculty hosts a real estate; the "link directory" picture was wrong

Earlier sampling suggested some faculties (ABP, FBE) were thin link directories. The full crawl disproves it: ABP runs 240 unique pages; FFAM a substantive 37-page section; MDHS 104 unique pages of clinical-placement compliance. What faculties do delegate is transactional admin (enrolment, fees, exams → hub). The duplication problem is smaller and thinner than feared; the seam problem is bigger.

3  Six URL conventions for one idea

"Information for current students" lives at six addresses: /students · /current-students · /study/current-students · /study/student-resources · /study/current-student-information · (and the hub). A student cannot guess their faculty's URL, and fbe…/current-students is a dead 404 where one would expect the page.

4  Careers is the clearest failure: parallel everywhere, connected nowhere

8 of 9 faculties plus MBS run their own careers/employability content in parallel with the central Careers hub — and hub and faculty careers pages do not cross-link in either direction. A student using the hub's career checklist may never discover their faculty's mentoring program, and vice-versa. This is the highest-value, lowest-cost fix in the estate.

5  The hub has real gaps where every faculty has built

The hub covers the transactional spine well but has no presence in services every faculty provides: placements & WIL (207 faculty pages; hub ≈ 0), degree-specific course planning, discipline academic skills, faculty forms (213 pages), and program regulatory pathways (LANTITE for teaching; Fitness-to-Practice / AHPRA for health). These gaps are why faculties build parallel sites.

6  School-level fragmentation multiplies the experience

Three schools run their own current-students sites beneath their faculty: MBS (61 pages, plus its own "Career Elevation" service), Biomedical Sciences (42), Dental (4). A Biomedicine student can traverse three different "home" experiences — the hub, the MDHS faculty section, and the Biomedical school site — each with a different URL and template.

7  Quality debt is visible and fixable

~50 pages are broken or misrouting, concentrated in Biomedical Sciences (18 — including 16 "major" pages with duplicate CMS slugs bouncing students to the prospective catalogue), FEIT (17), ABP (6), MDHS (3), Arts (3), FBE (2). The Dental site depends on case-sensitive paths (/Forms works, /forms 404s) and splits its own forms across two external form hosts.

8  No page tells students "what's where"

Not one faculty section carries a consistent "for X go to the hub; for Y stay here" signpost. Students learn the split by trial and error, and faculty pages routinely re-explain hub services (special consideration, leave of absence) that the hub owns definitively.

9  The topic evidence sharpens the consolidation target — and flags a data-quality clean-up

A topic-by-topic crawl (18 topics, full estate) pinpoints where consolidation pays. The transactional spine (enrolment, exams & results, special consideration, fees, library — 227 pages) has only 6 pages on the hub domain; the rest are faculty restatements of content the hub already owns — the single clearest target. Scholarships (faculties hold 141 pages of generic process scaffolding while a central catalogue already exists) and the duplicated "Employability in X" and "Wellbeing + Ambassadors" landing pages are next.

The same pass shows the topic page-counts are inflated by content-quality artifacts — ABP/MSD's ~700-page design-studio archive, FEIT nesting its whole estate under /orientation and /students/it paths, and ~70 Law researcher-profile pages. A meaningful share of the apparent "volume" is archival bloat and URL/CMS hygiene to fix at source, independent of the unification decision. (Full topic breakdown: Appendix B.)

04  The service-model verdict

The hub-and-faculty division of labour is the right one — but it is being run without the connective tissue that makes it work.

The hub is the correct owner of the transactional spine; faculties are the correct owners of placements, course plans, discipline skills and forms. What's missing is everything between them: consistent addressing, mutual signposting, a hub presence for placements and course planning, connected careers, consolidated school sites, and enforced standards. The fix is therefore not re-drawing ownership — it is engineering the seams.

05  Discussion points for DEAG

  • Autonomy vs consistency. Faculties have invested years in useful content. Standards must be mandated (URL chaos proves advisory guidance failed) without gutting ownership. How much standardisation, with what authority?
  • The 85%-unique reality. It takes "lift-and-shift to the hub" off the table. Does DEAG accept the goal is a connected federation, not a single site?
  • School-level sites (MBS, Biomedical, Dental). Tolerate, standardise, or consolidate into their faculty?
  • The careers silo. The clearest single win — who owns the hub↔faculty bridge, and will faculties embed the central tools?
  • Hub gaps. Filling placements / course-planning means the central team owns new content. Capacity to own it, or thin "connector" pages into faculty content?
  • Governance teeth. Standards as policy with audit and consequences, or another advisory guideline?
  • Personalisation as an accelerator. If Optimizely + Tealium (CDP) are in the stack, a personalised hub could surface the right faculty content to a known student without moving pages — potentially leapfrogging migration. Needs confirming.

07  The decision asked of DEAG

  • Decision 1 — Endorse the direction (C/D), ruling out full centralisation and status quo.
  • Decision 2 — Approve the no-regrets actions to begin immediately (below). Visible improvement within a quarter; no structural change.
  • Decision 3 — Mandate a standards baseline and commission a scoped Phase 2 (hub gap-fill + school-site consolidation policy + governance with audit). Confirm whether Optimizely/Tealium are available to determine C vs D.
