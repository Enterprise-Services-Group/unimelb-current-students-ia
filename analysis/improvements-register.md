## Executive summary

The current-student journey is not served by one estate but by **20+ public systems with no shared information architecture or vocabulary** — and the link graph shows the sprawl is far deeper than the headline suggests: **415 distinct `*.unimelb.edu.au` hosts** appear in the contextual graph alone (Fine-Arts cohort-year microsites, one-lab-one-subdomain research sites, 19 single-purpose transaction `*.app` hosts), with no registry tying any host to an owner or a retirement date. Across the four faculty domains, current students are only a **~4–7% tenant** of estates that are built for reputation, research and recruitment; **no two faculties share a top-level taxonomy**, and the student entry is variously a top-level `/students`, a `/current-students`, or a section buried 2+ levels inside the prospective `/study` funnel. The two ends of the lifecycle are barely wired: after stripping nav chrome, **only ~97 contextual links reach the alumni estate** (it is nav-only, with no in-content graduation→alumni handoff) and **only ~100 reach central Careers Online** — the estate's two weakest seams. Meanwhile the canonical hub, `students.unimelb.edu.au` (833 pages, not the "74" the deck asserts), is itself **mid-migration**, dual-serving ~120 leaf pages under two parallel URL trees, and the estate's biggest single dependency — the Handbook (linked 3,380+ times, 7,819 contextual inbound) — sits on a different platform, governed to a visibly lower web standard. The opportunity is therefore overwhelmingly **structural and governance-led, not visual**: a thin standardised student overlay on the Squiz platform the University *already runs everywhere*, a subdomain/forms registry to stop re-fragmentation, completion of the in-flight hub migration, and a set of high-leverage template/redirect fixes that each clear thousands of links at once.

> **Important data caveat (carried from `content-quality` finding):** the Handbook's "14% orphaned / thin" signal is substantially a **crawl artifact** — ~1,470 of 2,134 crawled handbook captures are Imperva/Incapsula bot-block shells (~887 bytes), and the 266 "orphans" are largely these blocked SPA pages. The *actionable* handbook issues (SPA with no server-rendered fallback, sub-baseline metadata, missing browsable indexes, no enrol action) stand on their own; the raw orphan/thinness percentages should be annotated as WAF/SPA artifacts and the handbook re-crawled before any "handbook is mostly thin" claim reaches the deck.

## Scope, confidence & what is NOT yet verified

This register is grounded in the **public** estate scrape (21,250 content pages, 20 domains, the path-level link graph). Five honesty boundaries a reviewer must carry forward before any item ships:

1. **The authenticated core is uncrawlable.** `my.unimelb` (SIS: enrolment, results, fees, timetable), Canvas/LMS, and the ~18 `*.app`/`forms.your` microsites sit behind login and appear only as outbound destinations. Every "wire X to my.unimelb / Careers Online / a form" recommendation is inferred from the public side — confirm the logged-in flow doesn't already do it (request screenshots / a walkthrough).
2. **All 404 / redirect / "phantom-404" claims are URL-string inference, not live HTTP.** Nothing in this project issued an HTTP request. The `?a=` artifact (2.3), the dead-link sweep (2.4), the www-vs-bare pairs (5.4) and the parallel-tree leaves (1.1) need **HEAD-probe confirmation** of real status codes before remediation is scoped.
3. **Findings are structural-evidence-only, not CX-validated** (carries `data-and-synthesis-issues.md` D6). The "add a bridge / CTA" items (3.1, 3.2, 8.x) rest on link-graph absence, not observed student failure — validate the costliest seams with real users.
4. **No analytics / traffic data.** Link-volume is the importance proxy throughout; it is not demand. The internal `in_c=` tracking params in the crawled URLs imply GA/search-log data exists — **requesting it is the single highest-value next step**, so priority can follow pages students actually hit.
5. **Two evidence notes:** near-duplicate claims are now reproducible via `analysis/full-scrape/near_dup.py` (4.x corrected below); the Handbook "14% orphaned" figure is largely an Incapsula bot-block artifact pending a JS-enabled re-crawl; and `students.unimelb` is **833 pages crawled / 679 with substantive content after de-dup** (both figures correct, different denominators — neither is the deck's "74").

## 1. UX & Information Architecture

### 1.1 Finish the stalled hub migration & collapse the parallel URL trees `[HIGH · medium]`
*(merges ia-navigation "dual-serves 120 leaf pages" + task-journeys "Collapse the parallel URL trees")*
The canonical hub is mid-migration and runs **two parallel trees for the same content**. A query over `crawl/students-full/index.json` (833 pages) reproduces **120 leaf page-names living under 2+ top-level roots**: 75 under both `/your-course` AND `/course-admin`, 24 under both `/student-support` AND `/support-and-wellbeing`, plus a tail. The pairs are near-identical live pages with distinct fingerprints, not redirects (e.g. `/your-course/.../academic-progress` 240,879 b fp aa9d8e88 vs `/course-admin/academic-progress` 240,595 b fp f32306f0; Stop 1 served at both `/student-support/advice-and-help/stop-1` and `/support-and-wellbeing/stop-1`). The split shows up as competing inbound links: the old Stop 1 path is linked **1,648×** estate-wide vs **224×** on the new path; the old visa `advanced-standing` path **613×** vs **303×** new. The hub actually exposes 11 substantive top-level roots, not the "four" the corpus asserts (structural-findings.md Finding 23) — the lean exemplar held up to standardise faculties against is itself the most internally-inconsistent IA in the estate.
**Do:** Pick one canonical root per concept (the analytics-winning URL — data suggests `/support-and-wellbeing` and `/your-course` are the newer targets), 301-redirect all 120 legacy leaves, add `rel=canonical`, and update the high-fan-in links (the 1,648 to old Stop 1, 613 to old visa). Add a redirect-coverage check so no path has live inbound on both trees. **This must land FIRST** — faculties are being told to link into a hub whose own URLs are unstable. **Acceptance test:** re-run the dual-path leaf query; target 0 leaves under 2+ roots (currently 120).

### 1.2 One URL convention for the current-students entry — top-level `/students` `[HIGH · medium]`
A "first segment containing *student*" query confirms **five conventions at different depths**: depth-0 `/students` (eng 222, law 201, arts 92, science 67, fbe 43), depth-0 `/current-students` (msd 993, ffam 47), and **depth-2+ buried inside the prospective `/study` funnel** (mdhs `/study/current-students` 17, medicine `/study/current-student-resources` 16, biomed `/study/current-student-information` 42, dental `/study/student-profiles` 5; education buries 107 under `/study/graduate-research/current-students`). A student's current-students home is sometimes a top-level nav item and sometimes 2+ clicks deep inside prospective content.
**Do:** Mandate top-level `/students` (the modal pattern, 5 faculties) and 301-redirect `/current-students`, `/study/current-students`, `/study/current-student-information/-resources`, `/study/student-profiles` to it. Pull the MDHS/medicine/biomed/dental student sections OUT of `/study` (prospective) up to `/students` (enrolled). Redirect + nav-config task, not a content rebuild.

### 1.3 No two faculties share a top-level taxonomy — there is no common IA to standardise *into* `[HIGH · large]`
First-path-segment analysis of all 12 faculty `index.json` files: the dominant top-level section differs in **every** domain and is almost never the student section — mdhs 89% `/research` (5,387/6,072), medicine 63% `/school-structure` (2,015/3,213), ffam 51% `/about-us`, law 49% `/centres`, dental 47% `/engage`, science 43% `/engage`, msd 42% `/current-students`, biomed 30% `/departments`, fbe 23% `/newsroom`, eng 21% `/mric`, education 20% `/faces-of-education`, arts 13% `/school-of-social-and-political-sciences`. Section *count* ranges 9 (science, dental) → 54 (arts). This is the parent-architecture root cause of the five-URL-convention problem.
**Do:** Do **not** try to unify parent faculty taxonomies (they serve research/news/industry, not students). Mandate a thin, consistent **student overlay** every faculty exposes identically regardless of parent IA: a fixed `/students` path, a fixed set of section labels, and a fixed "what's here vs what's on students.unimelb" block. The only lever that touches all 12 architectures without a rebuild — and it depends on the hub IA being stable first (1.1).

### 1.4 Research-led faculties demote the student entry below research `[MEDIUM · large]`
The three medical-cluster domains the corpus's 9-faculty cut omitted are the most extreme: mdhs 89% `/research` with students at `/study/current-students` (17 pp); medicine 63% `/school-structure` with students at `/study/current-student-resources` (16 pp); biomed 30% `/departments` + large `/sbs-research-groups` tail with students at `/study/current-student-information` (42 pp). These three also contribute almost nothing to the hub (medicine 21, mdhs 175, biomed 0 contextual links into students.unimelb) — confirming the "research paradox" (biggest research estates carry least student support) at the IA level.
**Do:** For mdhs, medicine, biomed, the overlay mandate must promote the student entry to a stable top-level `/students` node OUT of the research/study hierarchy, plus a persistent global-nav "Current students" link. Treat these three as the priority pilot — their fragmentation cost is highest.

### 1.5 Navigation depth has no estate standard — content buried 9–11 levels deep `[MEDIUM · medium]`
URL-depth histogram over the 12 faculty `index.json` files: mean path depth 3.12 (mdhs) → 5.36 (msd) and 5.06 (medicine), max 9–11 (eng 11, law 10, biomed 10, msd 9, medicine 9). The deep tail is concentrated in marketing-led estates: MSD 397 pages at depth 6 and 888 at depth 7–8; medicine 560 at depth 6 and 716 at depth 7–8; law 507 at depth 6. `nav-depth.csv` corroborates (msd 65% of pages ≥5 deep, medicine 59%, study.unimelb 58%).
**Do:** Add a depth target (2–3 levels for task content, hard cap ~5) to the overlay template; prioritise re-nesting MSD's `/current-students` tree (993 pp) and Medicine's `/school-structure` (2,015 pp). Pair with a breadcrumb requirement so deep pages stay orientable.

## 2. Findability & Content Connections

### 2.1 MSD's 8,069 deep-links point at the legacy paths the hub is abandoning — a mass link-break waiting to fire `[HIGH · quick-win]`
`cross-site-flow.csv` row 1: msd → students = **8,069** contextual links, far above the next faculty (education 275, eng 224, arts 191, mdhs 175, fbe 131, law 99, science 64, biomed 0). 1,550 of 1,560 MSD pages (99%) carry the same ~8 templated deep links, each on the **legacy** top-level path (`/your-course`, `/student-support`) the hub is migrating off; only 50 MSD pages link the new `/support-and-wellbeing/stop-1`. MSD is simultaneously the best-integrated faculty and the most exposed to the unfinished migration.
**Do:** (1) When 1.1 lands, guarantee permanent 301s on all legacy `/your-course` and `/student-support` URLs so MSD's ~8,000 links keep resolving; (2) MSD maintains its hub links in ONE shared include so re-pointing them is a single edit, not 1,550; (3) audit the inverse problem — most faculties barely deep-link the hub (biomed 0, science 64, law 99), so canonical task pages are near-invisible from most faculty estates (see 4.x service model).

### 2.2 Surface live 2026 handbook subjects/courses via browsable indexes + give them a `<title>` `[HIGH · medium]`
*(merges findability "browsable indexes" + lifecycle "266 orphaned subject/course pages")*
`orphans.csv`: 266 of 289 estate orphans (92%) are handbook pages — **161 `/2026/subjects/` + 105 `/2026/courses/`**, every one a current-year node, all with a **blank `<title>`**, reachable only via the handbook search box (the estate's #1 hub is a handbook `/search` URL with 5,529 contextual inbound). Inbound study links land on course/overview pages, but the constituent subject pages a continuing student needs (to check a prerequisite or plan next semester) are dead-ends. **Caveat:** per the data-integrity finding, many of these 266 are Incapsula bot-block shells — confirm via a JS-enabled re-crawl, but the underlying *browse-reachability* gap is real (the handbook is a client-rendered SPA with no crawlable index).
**Do:** Generate crawlable A–Z / by-discipline / by-level index pages for the current year (e.g. a `/2026/subjects` index linking every subject and course node) and link them from the handbook home and each faculty's "courses" landing page; add `<title>` tags to the 2026 subject/course templates. Fix internal cross-linking so every subject is reachable from its parent course/major and from sibling subjects (prereq/co-req back-links), replacing JS-only subject widgets with real `<a>` links. Worklist: the 266 handbook rows of `orphans.csv`.

### 2.3 Fix the `?a=` artifact manufacturing phantom 404s on live pages `[HIGH · quick-win]`
The root cause behind the estate's apparent broken-link mass. 32 paths return "Page not found" **only** when a trailing `?a=` is appended (medicine `/school-structure?a=`, msd `/maker-spaces?a=`, biomed `/departments?a=`) — but the canonical paths are **live** (medicine `/school-structure` is a working 187KB "Departments at Melbourne Medical School"). Because URL normalisation strips the query, the `?a=` 404 twin merges with the canonical path and tags it dead. Of 4,316 contextual links that appear to hit 404s, **3,359 (78%) actually point at 30 destinations whose canonical URL is live** and 404 only via `?a=` — including the 1,143 in-content "Our Departments" links on Medicine. `hubs.csv` lists `medicine/school-structure?a=` as the #2 estate "hub" (1,146) purely from this artifact.
**Do:** Find the template/component emitting the trailing `?a=` (an unresolved anchor/analytics token) and remove it, or add a server rule stripping an empty `a` param before routing. Single fix clears ~3,359 phantom-broken links and collapses ~32 phantom-404 "hubs" back into live pages. No page restoration needed.

### 2.4 Sweep the ~414 genuinely-dead destinations (957 links) — after the `?a=` fix `[MEDIUM · medium]`
After separating the `?a=` artifact, the residual genuinely-dead surface is **957 contextual links → 419 destinations** (only 5 synthetic `/404` endpoints; ~414 real gaps, thin long tail — top is `msd/404` itself at 67, then ≤8 each). Corpus-wide 493 error-titled pages (title scan of 27,302 pages; `dead-pages.csv` lists 499), concentrated in the MDHS cluster (biomedical 153, msd 74, fbe 72, mdhs 53, medicine 51). A few are live failures the CMS still links to: law `mls-staff-listing-records/.../judith-grbich` (404), `.../dudi-rulliadi` (502), mdhs `diversity-and-inclusion/.../health-and-wellbeing` (502), research `commercialisation/researchers/our-entrepreneurial-ecosystem` (502).
**Do:** Run the `?a=` fix first; then a one-off sweep over the ~414 dead destinations — restore or 301 each, fix the referring template. Prioritise by faculty CMS (long tail, not a top-20). Stand up a recurring broken-link report keyed on `title='Page not found'`, **reported against canonical URLs** so `?a=` twins don't re-inflate it.

### 2.5 Stop treating search-result URLs as canonical destinations `[HIGH · large]`
The estate's #1 contextual hub is a paginated handbook **search** URL (`/search?page=27&types[]=course&year=2026`, 5,529 inbound). **13,435 contextual links point INTO search/results/listing pages**, and 6,040 crawled pages ARE listing pages (~28% of true content). Other faculty "hubs" are frozen search states: mdhs `/visit/research-library` "Search" (929), finearts news-topics query (881), fbe `/newsroom` with a 7-param query string (853).
**Do:** Establish canonical content pages for high-traffic intents (course discovery, news-by-topic, scholarships listing) and link to **those**, not to a frozen search state. Add `rel=canonical` on results pages pointing at a clean landing page; strip tracking/filter params from outbound template links; governance rule: in-content links must target a stable content URL, never a paginated/filtered search result.

### 2.6 Non-descriptive anchor text on the Handbook — the biggest single accessibility + findability win for the effort `[MEDIUM · quick-win]`
*(merges findability "non-descriptive link labels" + governance "link-quality standards" for the handbook case)*
`anchor-text.csv` for the handbook: **"View detailed information in the Handbook" ×2,909**, "Handbook" 651, "University Handbook" 217, raw URL 108, "Arrow-rightHandbook link" 102, "here" 27. Almost none name the destination — a correctly-named anchor like `LAWS50023 Legal Method and Reasoning` appears only **16×**. Same pattern on other key destinations: findanexpert linked **267× as "(no text)"** (vs 204 "Find an Expert"); www.unimelb 496× "(no text)". Screen-reader users get a list of identical "Handbook"/"here" links (WCAG 2.4.4).
**Do:** Estate content rule: links to the Handbook must use the subject/course name as anchor text. This is a find-and-replace-scale CMS template fix on the ~2,900 templated "View detailed information in the Handbook" instances.

### 2.7 Fix non-descriptive in-content link labels across the estate `[MEDIUM · medium]`
Measured strictly over the estate's own contextual graph (chrome-stripped, unimelb-host): **~3,300 contextual links carry vague/missing labels** — 1,805 empty/no-text, 790 bare-URL-as-text, plus ~700 generic ("read more" 833, "here" 403, "find out more" 299). *(Correction: the prior 30,722/13,794-empty figure conflated external links — 11,989 of the empty-text links point to non-unimelb social/partner icons, a separate external-link-accessibility pass.)*

Add a content-authoring lint rule blocking "click here"/"read more"/empty link text. Start with findanexpert and www.unimelb. Treat the ~12k empty external icon links as a footer/icon-component accessibility pass.

### 2.8 Retire stale duplicate `…2` school pages on mdhs `[MEDIUM · quick-win]`
All 6 mdhs orphans are duplicate `…2`-suffixed school landing pages — `/our-organisation/melbourne-medical-school2`, `/melbourne-school-of-health-sciences2`, `/melbourne-dental-school2`, `/melbourne-school-of-psychological-sciences2`, `/melbourne-school-of-population-and-global-health2`, `/school-of-biomedical-sciences2` — each a full school homepage with ZERO contextual inbound, diluting relevance against the canonical pages.

## 3. Service Design, Lifecycle Seams & Ownership

### 3.1 Build the graduation→alumni bridge — stop handing finishing students to social media `[HIGH · quick-win]`
*(merges lifecycle "graduate→alumni transition seam" + task-journeys "graduation→alumni bridge")*
Python over `crawl/students-full` (833 pp): students.unimelb → **any alumni host = 0**. There are **35** true graduation/completion pages (`/graduation/graduation-day`, `/testamur`, `/australian-higher-education-graduation-statement-ahegs`, 16 ceremony-schedule pages, the Stop 1 graduation category page). The literal graduation-day page links out to Facebook (2), Instagram (2), LinkedIn (1) — but not to the alumni community, benefits, or `mentoring.unimelb.edu.au` (a live host, 59 references estate-wide, never linked from a graduation page). Estate-wide only 97 contextual links reach alumni hosts, and those are event RSVPs ("Register to attend" 13, "Book now" 10) — never a lifecycle handoff. The only faculty→alumni links that exist are **49 to a giving/donation "match" form**. "You are now a graduate" has no bridge to alumni identity. *(Correction: earlier "83 pages" double-counted; true count is 35; the 0-alumni-links result is unchanged.)*
**Do:** Add a standard "After you graduate / Your next chapter" block to the ~10 highest-traffic graduation pages (both URL-tree copies) linking to: alumni community sign-up (`www.unimelb.edu.au/alumni`), alumni benefits, and the alumni mentoring platform. ### 3.2 Wire faculty + hub to central Careers Online at the apply-for-jobs moment `[HIGH · medium]`

Target: lift faculty→careers to a floor of ~18 per faculty (the eng/FBE pattern).

### 3.4 Replicate the MSD central-hub model — 12 faculties under-route students vs MSD's 8,069 `[MEDIUM · large]`

### 3.5 Make Stop 1 a genuinely universal, evenly-routed front door `[MEDIUM · medium]`

*Evidence:* First-hand HTML analysis of all 154 pages >500KB in /crawl found ~9 pages shipping >100KB of base64-encoded images embedded directly in the HTML document (not as cacheable image files): finearts-music.unimelb.edu.au/about-us/news/meet-nicole-goode,-honours-visual-art-graduate = 2,292KB total, 2,071KB is a SINGLE inlined photo; mdhs.unimelb.edu.au/diversity-and-inclusion/initiatives/dei-conference = 2,704KB (2,501KB base64); msd.unimelb.edu.au/thus/news-events/urban-density-and-access-to-social-services = 1,779KB (1,617KB); mdhs .../mdhs-diversity-and-inclusion-grants = 1,220KB (1,054KB); law.unimelb.edu.au/news/MLS/from-education-to-precision-use = 1,145KB (976KB); biomedicalsciences .../cmr/about-us/contact-us = 734KB (570KB). page-weight.csv confirms these as the per-domain max outliers (finearts max 2,292KB, medicine 2,516KB, msd 1,779KB). **Do:** Add a CMS publish-time guard (and a crawl-based linter over freshness-signals tooling) that blocks/flags any inline data:image payload over ~10KB; convert offending images to served files with width-appropriate srcset, loading="lazy", and modern formats (WebP/AVIF). ### Student course template ships ~327KB of document weight before a single external file loads `[HIGH · medium]`
