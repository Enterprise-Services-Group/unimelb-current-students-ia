# Topic Deep-Dive: Careers & Employability
*Cross-faculty analysis from the full crawl (supersedes the earlier partial-crawl version). June 2026.*

**141 careers-employability–tagged pages** sit across the faculty estate — a mid-sized topic, but the most *evenly distributed* one in the audit: no single school dominates the way MBS owns course-planning. The headline pattern is **split and structurally duplicated** — every faculty independently maintains an "Employability in X" landing page and its own career-mentoring program, while the genuinely faculty-owned content is the discipline-specific stuff: regulated career pathways (becoming a barrister, teacher registration) and credit-bearing internship subjects.

> Deeper ESI analysis is held separately in the employability workspace.

| Unit | Pages | What dominates |
|---|--:|---|
| Law | 24 | Regulated legal-career pathways + ELLIS/mentoring |

Unlike course-planning, careers content is **spread almost flat across the top four faculties** (Law, Education, Arts, FEIT hold 88 of 141 pages between them). The tail is misleading: Biomedical's 17 are almost entirely thin redirects to `study.unimelb.edu.au` major pages, and FBE's "2 pages" are login stubs — so the *real* careers estate is smaller and more concentrated than the raw count suggests.

## Types of Careers & Employability content

1. **The "Employability in X" faculty landing page** — the clearest duplication pattern in the topic. Almost every faculty has one, structurally identical and often near-identical in copy: *Law* "Employability in Law", *Arts* "Employability in Arts", *Science* "Employability in Science", *FEIT* "Employability in Engineering and Information Technology", *MDHS* "Employability in MDHS" ("every experience and skill you build becomes part of your growing professional portfolio"), *FFAM* "Employability in Fine Arts and Music", *Education* "Employability in Education". Seven faculties, one template.

2. **Regulated / discipline-specific career pathways** — genuinely faculty-owned because they're bound to a profession's accreditation. *Law* is the deepest: "Steps to Practising Law" (1,556w — admission to practice, practising certificates), "Becoming a Barrister", "Admission to Practice", and a "Career Pathways" set (Public Interest & Government, Private Sector, Business, Judiciary, Research & Academia). *Education* treats the entire teaching-registration apparatus as employability — "Permission to Teach (PTT) Information", "Working with Children Check", placement requirements.

3. **Credit-bearing experiential learning & internship subjects** — coded subjects with a Handbook code, owned by the faculty that teaches them. *Arts* "Experiential Learning" ("learning by doing… applying knowledge in workplaces"), "Industry Project (ARTS30001)", "Internship: Personal & Career Growth", "Job Ready Program" (a 6-week series); *Science* "Internships in the Faculty of Science" (2,666w), "Industry projects for graduate students"; *ABP* "Internships and Vocational Placements", "ABPL90434 Construction Management Internship".

4. **Mentoring programs** — present in nearly every faculty, each branded and run locally: *Law* "MLS Mentor Program"; *Arts* "Arts Career Mentoring" (matches grad students with alumni industry experts); *FEIT* "Intercultural Career Mentoring Program (ICMP)" and "Peer Connect Program" (1,614w); *ABP* "ABP Industry Mentoring" (1,858w); *MDHS* "Mentoring Programs" (Academic, Chancellor's Scholars, Honours Alumni, Nursing, Peer); *FFAM* "Fine Arts and Music Career Mentoring"; *Education* "Career Mentoring" + "Peer Mentoring". The *kind* of program differs by discipline; the existence of one is universal.

5. **Student ambassador & leadership programs** — a recurring enrichment-as-employability category. *Arts* "Arts Student Ambassadors", *FEIT* "Student Ambassador Leadership Program (SALP)", *ABP* "Student Ambassador & Leadership Program" + "Construction Ambassador Program", *Education* "Student Ambassador program", *MDHS* "Student Leadership" + "Student Advisory Council".

6. **1:1 career consultations & practitioner-led services** — the transactional "book a careers appointment" layer. *Law* "1:1 Career Consultations" ("resume and cover letter review, job-searching strategies, key selection criteria review") and "Career Support for Graduate Researchers"; *FEIT* "Career resources" + "Professional Associations"; *Science* "Career Enrichment Program". This is the most hub-overlapping service type — the central Careers & Employability team runs the same offer.

7. **Professional-skills / industry-readiness series** — short structured programs distinct from credit subjects. *FEIT* "Professional Skills Series" (appears four times across different URL paths — three redirects, one live); *FFAM* "Ignite LAB" (1,439w, indexed twice); *Law* "ELLIS Program" (Experiential Learning for Law International Students) with per-year "2023"/"2024" sub-pages.

8. **Student & alumni career profiles** — testimonial content used as employability evidence. *Law* graduate-researcher profiles (Fia Hamid-Walker, Alumita Lekenaua, Jasmine Ali); *MDHS* Chancellor's Scholars profiles (Garry Zhu, Emily Sun, Zahra Ataie). Low-volume, high-page-count padding — each profile is its own URL.

A cross-cutting pattern worth flagging: **URL churn inflates this topic badly**. FEIT's "Intercultural Career Mentoring Program" exists at five different URLs and "Professional Skills Series" at four (mostly redirects); Arts indexes "Arts Student Ambassadors" twice and FFAM "Ignite LAB" twice. A meaningful share of the 141 is the same content under multiple paths, plus Biomedical's 16 redirect stubs and FBE's 2 login redirects.

## Legitimate vs hub-overlapping

| Faculty-owned (cannot be centralised) | Overlaps the hub (consolidation candidate) |
|---|---|
| Regulated career pathways (Law: barrister/admission; Education: PTT, registration) | The "Employability in X" landing page (7 near-identical copies) |
| Credit-bearing internship/experiential subjects (Arts, Science, ABP) | 1:1 career consultations / resume & cover-letter advice |
| Discipline-specific industry mentoring (ABP, FEIT ICMP, Law MLS) | Generic "career resources" / job-search-strategy pages |
| Profession-bound professional bodies (FEIT "Professional Associations") | Student-ambassador & general career-mentoring boilerplate |
| Faculty-run programs with their own brand (ELLIS, Ignite LAB, Job Ready) | Redirect/login stubs & duplicate-URL content (Biomedical, FBE, FEIT) |

The balance tilts more toward **hub-overlap than course-planning did**. The discipline-bound core (regulated pathways, coded internship subjects, industry mentoring) is legitimately faculty-owned — but the repeated "Employability in X" pages, generic consultation/resume services, and ambassador boilerplate are the same offer restated 7–10 times, on top of substantial URL-churn and redirect noise.

## Recommendation
- **Hub owns:** the canonical Careers & Employability service — appointment booking, resume/cover-letter/selection-criteria advice, job-search strategy, and the generic "what employability means at Melbourne" framing. This is one offer; it should have one home.
- **Faculty keeps:** regulated career pathways (Law, Education, MDHS), credit-bearing internship/experiential subjects (Arts, Science, ABP), and discipline-specific industry-mentoring programs with their own brands (ELLIS, Ignite LAB, ICMP, ABP Industry Mentoring).
- **Collapse the template:** the seven "Employability in X" pages should become *one* hub page + a thin per-faculty stub that links to the discipline-specific subjects and mentoring only — not a full restatement of the central service.
- **Add one hub gateway:** `students.unimelb.edu.au/careers/faculty-employability` — a directory linking to each faculty's internship subjects, mentoring, and regulated-pathway content, so discipline-specific careers material is discoverable from the centre.
- **Highest-value fix:** kill the URL churn and redirect stubs (FEIT's 5×/4× duplicate programs, Biomedical's 16 redirect pages, FBE's 2 login stubs). This alone removes a large fraction of the 141 without losing any real content, and makes the legitimate split legible.
