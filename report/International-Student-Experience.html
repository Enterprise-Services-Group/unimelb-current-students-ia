An end-to-end audit of the University's highest-stakes audience — where a web fault doesn't cost convenience, it costs a visa. Nine journey stages from discovery to post-study, traced across study.unimelb, the forked current-student support trees, and the external immigration and application systems. June 2026.

Executive summary

International students are the journey the University can least afford to get wrong, and the one where its estate-wide faults turn legally consequential. The work is, in parts, genuinely good — there is a purpose-built international decide-hub, CoE and OSHC guidance are well-written, eligibility self-serves for listed countries. But end to end, the journey has a single defining failure repeated at every compliance moment: the estate explains the rule but never connects it to the action, and the rules in question are the ones that keep a visa valid.

It compounds in three ways. First, the most immigration-critical content is forked across three live, simultaneously-published trees — a canonical /support-and-wellbeing tree, a deprecated /student-support tree that 12 domains still link, and a live /sandbox/2026-uplifts draft — so a student forms their visa picture from whichever fork they happen to land on. Second, the visa condition and the academic action never meet: the full-time study-load requirement that maintains the visa is invisible at the moment of enrolment, and the highest-risk pages ("reduced study load", "enrolment changes that affect your visa") link to zero action forms. Third, the biggest drawcards and the dominant channel are absent or undiscoverable: the post-study work visa (subclass 485) — the single largest reason many international students choose Australia — has no destination page anywhere on the estate, and the education-agent channel most international students actually apply through is content-rich but functionally undiscoverable and duplicated across two URLs.

There is also a quiet accuracy risk under all of it: the international fee and entry figures a student builds a visa financial-capacity plan on are dual-authored on a single domestic-shared page (with a still-live "2025" requirements fork), so a desync feeds a wrong number into a legally-tested decision. The fix is not more content — the content largely exists — it is closure: one canonical international spine, every condition wired to its action, the forks retired, and the absent drawcards (485, agents) actually built.

The recurring patterns — where the estate's faults turn legal

1. Know but can't act — at every compliance moment. CoE, OSHC, study-load, visa conditions and work limits are all described on guidance pages whose route to the action (eStudent, my.unimelb, the *.app forms, the deposit flow) is missing or behind a wall. The find→act break is the estate's signature fault; here each instance carries an immigration consequence.

2. The most critical content is forked across three live trees. International support runs on /support-and-wellbeing (canonical, 27), /student-support (deprecated, 12) and a live /sandbox/2026-uplifts draft (10) at once — and 12 domains link the deprecated one. On the estate's highest-stakes topic, the student may read the wrong fork.

3. The visa condition and the academic action never meet. The full-time study-load requirement that keeps the visa valid is invisible at the enrolment moment; the academic enrolment task (my.unimelb) and the visa rule (a guidance page) are managed in different places and never connected at the point of action.

4. The drawcards and the dominant channel are missing. The post-study work visa (485) — the biggest reason to choose Australia — has no page at all. The education-agent channel most international students apply through is duplicated across two byte-identical URLs and has no per-country authorised-agent verification. The estate under-builds exactly where international demand concentrates.

5. Accuracy is ungoverned on legally-tested fields. International fees and entry are dual-authored on one domestic-shared page, with a still-live "2025" English-requirements fork — and these feed the subclass-500 financial-capacity and eligibility tests. A silent desync is a compliance error, not a typo.

6. Everything decisive is off-estate. The application (eStudent), enrolment and CoE (my.unimelb), the visa (Home Affairs) and OSHC (provider sites) all happen behind walls or offshore. The public estate is an instruction manual for systems it cannot show — so the handoffs are where the journey lives, and they are exactly what isn't governed.

The nine stages, end to end

Discover & decide (international audience) [MEDIUM]

A genuine international DECIDE hub exists and is well-built — but underneath it is the domestic site with a fees toggle: course discovery, fees and entry are single dual-audience pages, there are ZERO country/region-specific journeys, and the agent / overseas-representative content is one page served under two URLs. Strong hub, generic depth.

What exists. Two faces. (1) A purpose-built DECIDE hub /student-life/international-students (dir dd9bb0da4b975a32, 51 outbound links) curating the 'why Melbourne for international' story: forward links to /discover, /student-life/lifestyle, /student-life/cost-of-living, /student-life/settling-in ('Tips from international students'), /campus-and-facilities, /accommodation, /connect-with-us/view-our-brochures, plus 3 international meet-our-students profiles (Gautam/Jegath/Winda). International-House accommodation = exactly 6 pages (/accommodation/international-house + about/how-to-apply/alumni/faqs/scholarships). (2) The COURSE-DISCOVERY layer is dual-audience-on-one-URL: /find = 3,345 pages; 420 unique courses, 341 carrying a SINGLE /fees/ and SINGLE /entry-requirements/ tab serving BOTH cohorts inline — e.g. Master of Data Science /fees/ (c7b2fee4f6c64528) renders domestic AUD44, 992first − year/94,484 total AND international AUD57, 984/121,767 on the same page, with and baked into the head. English/eligibility decide-content: /how-to-apply/english-language-requirements = 10 pages (UG + graduate + course-specific). The decide→apply tracks: /how-to-apply/{undergraduate-study,graduate-coursework-study,graduate-research,international-exchange-and-study-abroad}/international-applications, each with entry-requirements / applications / offers-and-enrolments / fees-and-payments (110 pages carry 'international' in the path). Agent channel: /how-to-apply/authorised-education-agent (36ece15a07d80710) + /how-to-apply/find-an-overseas-representative (368478b78b79f902).

Friction (immigration/compliance consequences flagged)

  • ELIGIBILITY/COMPLIANCE ACCURACY: international entry depends on per-course fee + IELTS figures that are dual-authored on a single domestic-shared page. The 341 /fees/ pages embed the international figure as alongside the domestic one — if the toggle/markup desyncs, an international prospect can read a domestic fee or a domestic-cohort entry score and build a visa/financial plan on the wrong number (the GTE/financial-capacity test for subclass 500 keys off this).
  • STALE ELIGIBILITY DATA: English-language-requirements carries a still-published '2025-english-language-requirements' page AND _nocache duplicates (/2025-english-language-requirements, /2025-english-language-requirements/_nocache) crawled in 2026 — an out-of-date IELTS/band threshold at the decide stage directly feeds a wrong eligibility judgement before the applicant ever reaches the portal.
  • DEPRECATED-TREE HANDOFF AT THE DECIDE STAGE: the international decide-hub's 'Read visa information' CTA points to students.unimelb.edu.au/student-support/international-student-support/visas — the DEPRECATED legacy support tree (canonical is /support-and-wellbeing/international-student-support). A visa-curious prospect is sent into a deprecated, possibly diverging tree at the exact moment they are forming the visa picture. Same hub also deep-links /student-support/international-student-support/under-18-international-student-supervision-program and /visas/visas-for-your-family into the same deprecated fork.
  • NO LOCALISED RISK/COST CONTEXT: cost-of-living and lifestyle decide-pages mention 'international' but carry NO visa context (visa=false in body) and no country-specific cost framing — a prospect cannot see the visa financial-capacity / OSHC cost reality during the decide stage; that only surfaces later, on the wrong (logged-out current-student) side of the wall per the existing applicant-journey profile.
  • AGENT CHANNEL UNDERSPECIFIED: the single agent page tells applicants they 'can apply through an authorised education agent' but the public estate has no per-country authorised-agent list rendered (the agent directory is generic) — for markets where agents are the dominant channel, the decide-stage trust signal (is THIS agent authorised?) is not resolvable on-site.

Fragmentation

  • ONE GENERIC LAYER, NOT LOCALISED JOURNEYS: ZERO country/region-specific landing pages exist (no /china, /india, /your-country pattern anywhere in 4,048 study.unimelb pages). The international audience gets a single global overlay; discover/decide content (cost-of-living, lifestyle, discover) is SHARED single pages, not internationally forked.
  • DUPLICATE-URL AGENT CONTENT: /authorised-education-agent and /find-an-overseas-representative are BYTE-IDENTICAL (both 9,100 chars of body text, identical hash) — one page published under two URLs. The 'thin (1 page) agent content' is really one asset double-counted, splitting inbound links and SEO signal across two addresses.
  • DUAL-AUDIENCE-ON-ONE-PAGE vs SEPARATE-TRACK SPLIT: international fees/entry are merged into the domestic course page (toggle model) at /find, but the application narrative is RE-AUTHORED per level (UG/GradCW/GradResearch each own a full international-applications subtree). So the same audience is sometimes a toggle and sometimes a separate tree — inconsistent mental model across the discover→apply boundary.
  • INBOUND EXCHANGE CO-MINGLED: /how-to-apply/international-exchange-and-study-abroad-applications (18 pages, its own tuition-fees/important-dates/getting-ready/returning-home) is a near-separate inbound-mobility PRODUCT living under the same /how-to-apply roof as the degree-seeking international journey — two different audiences, one shelf.
  • DECIDE CONTENT SPANS 4+ TOP-LEVEL SECTIONS: the international decide story is scattered across /student-life (lifestyle, cost-of-living, settling-in, meet-our-students), /discover, /accommodation/international-house, /how-to-apply (english, definitions, agent) and /connect-with-us (brochures) — assembled only by the curated hub's 51 hand-placed links, with no canonical 'International students' spine that owns the sequence.

The seam. The decide→apply handoff into the walled portal. The international-applications 'Applications' page (e7c2dd25f76922c6) narrates eStudent/eApplication and routes via the bridge page /how-to-apply/your-online-application (70ed708019098089), whose only real exit is the uncrawlable T1 eStudent portal: https://prod.ss.unimelb.edu.au/student/SM/eApplications/eAppLogin.aspx (with a t1cloud.com eStudent mirror). That seam is bridged cleanly. The HIDDEN seam is the SIDEWAYS one: while still deciding, the international hub pushes the visa/under-18/family-visa questions OUT of the recruitment estate and INTO students.unimelb's DEPRECATED /student-support/international-student-support tree — so the prospect crosses from study.unimelb into a logged-out, deprecated current-student fork before they have applied, and (per the existing applicant-journey profile) the same OSHC/CoE steps then resolve to a different URL per audience track downstream. Decide also reaches into handbook.unimelb for authoritative per-course entry/English detail (study→handbook = thousands of links), a third system the decide stage silently depends on.

Recommendation. Keep the strong /student-life/international-students decide-hub as the canonical 'International students' spine, but fix three depth gaps. (1) ACCURACY GUARDRAIL on the dual-audience course pages: treat fees_international + entry_requirements meta as a governed compliance field with an explicit 'last verified / year' stamp and an automated check that domestic and international figures cannot silently desync; retire the still-live 2025-english-language-requirements page and its _nocache duplicates so no stale IELTS threshold drives an eligibility judgement. (2) RE-POINT every decide-stage handoff (the hub's 'Read visa information', under-18, visas-for-your-family links) from the DEPRECATED /student-support/international-student-support/* tree to the canonical /support-and-wellbeing/international-student-support/* tree — a decide-stage prospect should never be seeded into a deprecated fork. (3) COLLAPSE the byte-identical /authorised-education-agent and /find-an-overseas-representative into one canonical URL with a 301 from the other, and add the visa financial-capacity / OSHC cost reality to the cost-of-living decide page so prospects see the true cost-of-decision before the portal — not after, on the wrong side of the enrolment wall. Net: the international audience is served distinctly at the hub, but below the hub it is the domestic estate with a fees toggle and no localisation; close the accuracy + deprecated-link gaps before adding country journeys.

Eligibility & English-language requirements (international) [MEDIUM]

Listed-country applicants CAN self-serve eligibility from on-page tables — but everyone else falls into an unscoped "case-by-case" human funnel, and a stale "2025" requirements fork is still live and still linked from the current 2026 hub.

What exists. study.unimelb /how-to-apply/english-language-requirements: spec count 10, but the 8 crawled URLs collapse to ~4 unique pages — a hub (ffb55c1f9159baed), an Undergraduate page (9d37416d259471e9), a Graduate page (8db0627eeaaffb74), and a year-stamped "2025 English language requirements" archive (46a726c7472d9294); the remainder are trailing-slash + /_nocache duplicates that inflate the count. The hub defines two routes: (a) "English-speaking background" exemption (citizen/10-yr resident of a "recognised English-speaking country" who completed secondary/tertiary education in English), and (b) accepted proficiency tests, linking out to 8 external provider sites (IELTS, IELTS One Skill Retake, TOEFL iBT, PTE Academic, Cambridge, LanguageCert, Michigan/MET). The UG page (2,500 words, 3 tables) carries a genuine per-country academic-equivalency table — UK GCE/Cambridge Pre-U/GCSE, Australia VCE, Canada (Alberta/BC/Ontario named individually), Germany, Hong Kong HKDSE, India CBSE/CISCE, Ireland, IB. The PG page (4,636 words, 10 tables) carries course-specific thresholds (6.5/7.0 dominate; "7.0" appears 89x) plus bridging pathways (UMELBP, Hawthorn-Melbourne). So for a LISTED country, eligibility IS self-serviceable on-page without contacting anyone.

Friction (immigration/compliance consequences flagged)

  • IMMIGRATION/COMPLIANCE: Non-standard or unlisted qualifications route to 'assessed on a case-by-case basis' (UG page) with NO transactional form, no SLA, no named assessor — it defaults to the generic site-wide 'Contact us'/Support-centre funnel (the only contact CTA on hub, UG and PG is the boilerplate 'How can we help?' block). An applicant whose country is not in the table cannot determine eligibility self-service; this gates everything downstream (offer -> CoE -> subclass-500 visa), and an applicant who guesses wrong wastes an intake cycle.
  • STALENESS/COMPLIANCE: A '2025 English language requirements' page (46a726c7472d9294, 3,441 words) is STILL LIVE and is STILL LINKED from the current hub (verified in links.json) as of today 2026-06-28. It mentions '2025' 6x and '2026' only 1x. An applicant can land on year-old thresholds and apply against superseded numbers — a high-stakes error when a 0.5-band miss means no offer and no visa.
  • EXEMPTION TRAP: The 'English-speaking background' rule reads as country-based but carries silent exclusions — e.g. Sri Lankan A-Levels are EXPLICITLY excluded from the exemption ('must satisfy ... by completing an approved English language proficiency test') and several Canada rows are gated to 'only for students completing the qualification in Canada'. These caveats are buried in table prose, easy to miss, and reversing a wrong assumption late is costly.
  • EXTERNAL-DEPENDENCY: 8 of the decision-critical links leave the estate to third-party test providers (ielts.org, ets.org/toefl, pearsonpte.com, cambridgeenglish.org, etc.). The University does not host its own at-a-glance comparison of equivalent scores across all accepted tests in one place; the applicant must reconcile thresholds across the PG page tables and each provider's own scale.
  • HANDOFF-INTO-WALLED-SYSTEM: Document submission points at 'eStudent', the 'application portal' and /how-to-apply/instructions-to-upload-documents, but the eApplications portal is walled/uncrawled — the public estate explains WHAT to upload but cannot show or validate the actual upload step where an eligibility error would surface.

Fragmentation

  • Year-forking: a deprecated '2025-english-language-requirements' page coexists with the canonical pages AND is linked from the live hub — the same anti-pattern as the three forked current-student support trees, now appearing on the prospective side.
  • URL duplication: each unique page exists in trailing-slash and /_nocache variants (8 crawled -> ~4 real), so 'the 10 pages' overstates the genuine content footprint and dilutes link equity / analytics across near-identical URLs.
  • UG vs PG split is sensible, but the country-equivalency authority lives ONLY on the UG page; the PG page is threshold/test-score-centric, so a graduate applicant relying on a school qualification for the exemption must cross-read the UG table — the academic-equivalency 'source of truth' is not co-located with the audience that also needs it.
  • Stage-to-stage seam break: the post-arrival Diagnostic English Language Assessment (DELA, 613 inbound links on services.unimelb — a real placement/condition for some commencing students) is NOT referenced anywhere on the prospective English pages, so the 'English requirement' story silently restarts after arrival with no forward signposting.

The seam. The decisive handoff is the "case-by-case basis" assessment for any qualification not in the on-page country table. For listed countries the page IS the decision; for everyone else the page hands off into an unscoped, formless human queue (the generic Support-centre/Contact funnel) and then into the WALLED eStudent eApplications portal (uncrawled) where documents are actually judged. The public estate guides the standard case well but provides no transactional path, SLA, or eligibility-checker for the edge case — and silently, the requirement re-emerges post-arrival as DELA, which the prospective pages never mention.

Recommendation. 1) UNPUBLISH / 301 the live '2025 English language requirements' page and remove its link from the hub — never expose year-forked thresholds; keep a single dated 'current requirements' page (highest-priority fix, direct visa-error risk). 2) Publish the full list of recognised English-speaking countries and an expanded equivalency table (move/duplicate the UG country table to a shared component visible from BOTH UG and PG), and add a lightweight 'check your country' chooser so non-listed applicants get a definitive 'you must test' answer without contacting anyone. 3) Replace the generic 'Contact us' fallback for case-by-case assessments with a named, SLA-bearing intake (form -> admissions queue) so edge-case eligibility is traceable. 4) Collapse the /_nocache and trailing-slash duplicates to one canonical URL each. 5) Add a one-line forward signpost from the prospective English pages to post-arrival DELA so the requirement story is continuous across the lifecycle. 6) Provide a single on-site cross-test score-equivalence table so applicants are not forced to reconcile 8 external provider scales themselves.

Application & education agents (international) [HIGH]

The agent channel that most international students actually use to apply is content-rich but functionally undiscoverable and duplicated across two URLs; the application itself silently forks across three systems, and the eStudent eApplications launch never appears in the public link graph at all.

What exists. Not the '1 thin page' the brief assumed: a ~27-page /how-to-apply international cluster on study.unimelb (crawled 24 Jun 2026), with fractured authority. AGENT CONTENT: /how-to-apply/authorised-education-agent (hash 36ece15a07d80710, 9,102 chars of real content) is substantive: sectioned nav (About / Who must apply / Find an agent / nomination form / FAQ), a live external agent directory (eap.ascentone.com/unimelb, linked 4x), a downloadable nomination/authorisation PDF (UM_EdAgentNomAuthorisationForm.pdf), fraud guidance ('Only the authorised education agents listed are trained, approved and permitted... genuinely authorised'), scope limits ('cannot influence selection decisions, guarantee admission, or waive entry requirements'), and CoE/offer scope ('assisting you in accepting an offer... including through the provision of the conditional CoE process'). PORTAL-SEAM SHELL: /how-to-apply/your-online-application (hash 70ed708019098089, 264KB) + /your-online-application/application-help ('Application portal help') + /instructions-to-upload-documents + /fees/application-fee + /indicative-turn-around-times + /conditional-confirmation-of-enrolment. DEFINITIONS GATE: /domestic-and-international-student-definitions (hash b0d344d257df33b8) cleanly defines the domestic/international line (citizenship, PR, temporary resident, dual citizenship) that determines the entire downstream visa journey. study.unimelb is the heaviest estate (median 304KB; agent page ~245KB), slowest to load for the offshore/mobile audience that depends on it most.

Friction (immigration/compliance consequences flagged)

  • [COMPLIANCE/IMMIGRATION] The MANDATORY-agent rule is buried and never reaches the people it binds. The agent page states verbatim: 'Pakistan: All graduate degree applicants', 'India: Any international applicant who completed tertiary studies in India', 'China: All applicants who last studied in China can either apply through an authorised education agent or share their academic transcripts using the CSSD'. For these cohorts the agent channel is not optional but the ONLY valid application path; getting it wrong means an invalid application, delayed or withdrawn offer, and a knock-on delay to the CoE and the subclass-500 student visa.
  • [DISCOVERABILITY] The agent page is functionally undiscoverable via in-text links: anchor-text.csv contains ZERO contextual anchors reading 'education agent' or 'overseas representative' anywhere in the 20-domain estate. It is reachable essentially only by drilling the /how-to-apply left-nav, so an applicant who does not already know to look will never be told the mandatory channel exists.
  • [PORTAL SEAM] The actual 'Apply now / log in to your application' launch into eStudent eApplications is JS-injected and absent from the crawlable link graph. A whole-estate scan of study.unimelb links.json surfaces no eStudent/eApplicant/prod.ss launch URL; the only application-system URL exposed is studentit.unimelb.edu.au/news/troubleshooting-application-estudent-portal (a troubleshooting article). The 'Apply now' anchors that DO exist in anchor-text.csv point to unrelated systems (mentoring.unimelb x7, ctrs.app.unimelb x4), not the application portal. The single most decisive click in the funnel is invisible to navigation analysis and unguided in the static estate.
  • [CATALOGUE BLIND SPOT] Per-course 'How to apply' pages (the dominant entry path, ~128 instances across ~2,944 catalogue pages) do not surface the agent rule. Sampled Doctor of Medicine /how-to-apply (hash 8a09ed3b385e7981) links to /your-online-application but contains NO link to /authorised-education-agent or /find-an-overseas-representative. An India/Pakistan/China applicant browsing by course is routed straight to the portal and never told they must apply through an agent.
  • [BROKEN ANCHORS] The agent page's own in-page section links are malformed with double-hash hrefs ('##navigation-find-an-agent', '##navigation-agent-form'), so the on-page jump links to 'Find an agent in your country' and the nomination/authorisation form do not resolve.
  • [LOAD/EQUITY] At ~245KB the agent page (and the 264KB portal page) is among the heaviest in the estate, served to the audience most likely on constrained offshore/mobile connections at the highest-stakes moment.

Fragmentation

  • DUPLICATE URLs, one page: /how-to-apply/authorised-education-agent (36ece15a07d80710) and /how-to-apply/find-an-overseas-representative (368478b78b79f902) are byte-for-byte identical content (both 9,102 chars cleaned, identical text == True; meta byteLength 245,569 vs 245,575) under the SAME title 'Find an authorised education agent or representative'. This is why prior analysis counted '1 page' twice. Two URLs split link equity, dilute search authority, and create canonical ambiguity at the journey's most compliance-sensitive page.
  • THREE-WAY APPLICATION FORK (corroborated by prospective-experience-profiles.json): the apply action splits silently across VTAC (UG domestic), the walled eStudent eApplications portal (international + graduate), and a CRM enquiry form. study.unimelb explains the fork only via prose cross-links (e.g. /your-online-application links 'apply via VTAC' for UG domestic), with no single decision page that says 'which system applies to YOU'.
  • PORTAL HELP SCATTER: post-submit guidance is spread across at least four sibling pages (your-online-application, application-help, instructions-to-upload-documents, indicative-turn-around-times) plus an off-estate studentit troubleshooting article on a different subdomain, rather than consolidated at the point of the portal handoff.
  • TWO online-application URL variants crawled (70ed708019098089 root + 91d325dcd9a96c93 /application-help), and the conditional-CoE step lives as its own thin page (hash c2633aa692ce1721) outside the agent/portal cluster.

The seam. The apply -> portal handoff. The public estate carries the applicant up to the threshold (agent directory at eap.ascentone.com/unimelb; document-upload, application-fee, turnaround, and conditional-CoE pages) and then hands off into the walled eStudent eApplications portal via a launch action that is NOT in the crawlable link graph (JS-injected; only a troubleshooting article is statically linked). A parallel external seam runs through the AscentOne agent directory and the CSSD/CHSI transcript service (chsi.com.cn). For the mandatory-channel cohorts (India, Pakistan, China) the seam is doubly fragile: the applicant must FIRST find an agent via a page nothing in-text links to, THEN that agent drives the eStudent submission and conditional-CoE acceptance on their behalf, so the University's public guidance goes dark exactly when the highest-immigration-risk transaction begins.

Recommendation. 1) CANONICALISE the duplicate immediately: 301 /find-an-overseas-representative to /authorised-education-agent (or set rel=canonical) to consolidate authority on the single compliance-critical page. 2) SURFACE the mandatory-agent rule where applicants actually are: inject an 'If you studied in India / Pakistan / China you must apply through an authorised education agent' callout into every per-course /how-to-apply page and into /your-online-application BEFORE the portal launch, each deep-linking the agent directory. 3) MAKE THE PORTAL SEAM VISIBLE AND OWNED: add an explicit, statically-linked 'Start / log in to your application (eStudent)' CTA plus a one-screen 'which application system applies to you' decision page (VTAC vs eStudent vs agent vs enquiry) so the three-way fork is resolved before the handoff, not after. 4) FIX the malformed double-hash in-page anchors on the agent page. 5) ADD a short post-submit roadmap at the seam (offer -> deposit -> conditional then full CoE -> visa -> OSHC) so applicants understand what the portal triggers next. 6) WEIGHT BUDGET: trim the ~245-264KB page weight on these offshore-critical pages.

Offer, CoE & the ESOS framework (international legal core: accept offer -> tuition deposit -> conditional vs full CoE -> CoE, the ESOS document without which there is no subclass-500 visa) [HIGH]

CoE content is genuinely good, but the ESOS/Tuition Protection framework underwriting the AUD$17,000 deposit is an orphan page no offer flow links to; and the CoE transaction is forked across a canonical path, a deprecated tree, and a LIVE published sandbox draft.

What exists. Split across two domains at the lifecycle boundary. study.unimelb owns acceptance: /how-to-apply/{undergraduate-study|graduate-coursework-study}/international-applications/offers-and-enrolments (51-52 links each) explain offer->accept->deposit->OSHC->CoE in correct sequence; /undergraduate-study/international-applications/fees-and-payments states the AUD$17,000 deposit and links the binding Terms&Conditions PDF (2026-Tuition-Fees_Terms-and-Conditions-for-International-Students_Full_Degrees.pdf); a strong standalone /how-to-apply/conditional-confirmation-of-enrolment (35 links) fully explains conditional->full CoE conversion + eligibility. students.unimelb owns the operational CoE: /new-students/get-started/undergraduate-international/coe (Step 2, 28 links) gives the 10-working-day timeline and that CoE is required before administrative enrolment; canonical ESOS page /support-and-wellbeing/international-student-support/education-services-for-overseas-students-esos (30 links) correctly cites ESOS Act 2000 and links Tuition Protection Service (tps.gov.au), National Code 2018, the ESOS Act, and Fee Policy (MPF1325). The CoE document itself, the Acceptance & Payment Agreement, and deposit payment are all handoffs into the uncrawlable eStudent/my.unimelb portal - narrated but not transactable on the public estate.
