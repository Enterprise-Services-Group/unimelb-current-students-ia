Executive summary

The current-student journey is not served by one estate but by 20+ public systems with no shared information architecture or vocabulary — and the link graph shows the sprawl is far deeper than the headline suggests: 415 distinct *.unimelb.edu.au hosts appear in the contextual graph alone (Fine-Arts cohort-year microsites, one-lab-one-subdomain research sites, 19 single-purpose transaction *.app hosts), with no registry tying any host to an owner or a retirement date. Across the four faculty domains, current students are only a ~4–7% tenant of estates that are built for reputation, research and recruitment; no two faculties share a top-level taxonomy, and the student entry is variously a top-level /students, a /current-students, or a section buried 2+ levels inside the prospective /study funnel. The two ends of the lifecycle are barely wired: after stripping nav chrome, only ~97 contextual links reach the alumni estate (it is nav-only, with no in-content graduation→alumni handoff) and only ~100 reach central Careers Online — the estate's two weakest seams. Meanwhile the canonical hub, students.unimelb.edu.au (833 pages, not the "74" the deck asserts), is itself mid-migration, dual-serving ~120 leaf pages under two parallel URL trees, and the estate's biggest single dependency — the Handbook (linked 3,380+ times, 7,819 contextual inbound) — sits on a different platform, governed to a visibly lower web standard. The opportunity is therefore overwhelmingly structural and governance-led, not visual: a thin standardised student overlay on the Squiz platform the University already runs everywhere, a subdomain/forms registry to stop re-fragmentation, completion of the in-flight hub migration, and a set of high-leverage template/redirect fixes that each clear thousands of links at once.

Important data caveat (carried from content-quality finding): the Handbook's "14% orphaned / thin" signal is substantially a crawl artifact — ~1,470 of 2,134 crawled handbook captures are Imperva/Incapsula bot-block shells (~887 bytes), and the 266 "orphans" are largely these blocked SPA pages. The actionable handbook issues (SPA with no server-rendered fallback, sub-baseline metadata, missing browsable indexes, no enrol action) stand on their own; the raw orphan/thinness percentages should be annotated as WAF/SPA artifacts and the handbook re-crawled before any "handbook is mostly thin" claim reaches the deck.

Scope, confidence & what is NOT yet verified

This register is grounded in the public estate scrape (21,250 content pages, 20 domains, the path-level link graph). Five honesty boundaries a reviewer must carry forward before any item ships:

  1. The authenticated core is uncrawlable. my.unimelb (SIS: enrolment, results, fees, timetable), Canvas/LMS, and the ~18 *.app/forms.your microsites sit behind login and appear only as outbound destinations. Every "wire X to my.unimelb / Careers Online / a form" recommendation is inferred from the public side — confirm the logged-in flow doesn't already do it (request screenshots / a walkthrough).
  2. All 404 / redirect / "phantom-404" claims are URL-string inference, not live HTTP. Nothing in this project issued an HTTP request. The ?a= artifact (2.3), the dead-link sweep (2.4), the www-vs-bare pairs (5.4) and the parallel-tree leaves (1.1) need HEAD-probe confirmation of real status codes before remediation is scoped.
  3. Findings are structural-evidence-only, not CX-validated (carries data-and-synthesis-issues.md D6). The "add a bridge / CTA" items (3.1, 3.2, 8.x) rest on link-graph absence, not observed student failure — validate the costliest seams with real users.
  4. No analytics / traffic data. Link-volume is the importance proxy throughout; it is not demand. The internal in_c= tracking params in the crawled URLs imply GA/search-log data exists — requesting it is the single highest-value next step, so priority can follow pages students actually hit.
  5. Two evidence notes: near-duplicate claims are now reproducible via analysis/full-scrape/near_dup.py (4.x corrected below); the Handbook "14% orphaned" figure is largely an Incapsula bot-block artifact pending a JS-enabled re-crawl; and students.unimelb is 833 pages crawled / 679 with substantive content after de-dup (both figures correct, different denominators — neither is the deck's "74").

1. UX & Information Architecture

1.1 Finish the stalled hub migration & collapse the parallel URL trees [HIGH · medium]

(merges ia-navigation "dual-serves 120 leaf pages" + task-journeys "Collapse the parallel URL trees") The canonical hub is mid-migration and runs two parallel trees for the same content. A query over crawl/students-full/index.json (833 pages) reproduces 120 leaf page-names living under 2+ top-level roots: 75 under both /your-course AND /course-admin, 24 under both /student-support AND /support-and-wellbeing, plus a tail. The pairs are near-identical live pages with distinct fingerprints, not redirects (e.g. /your-course/.../academic-progress 240,879 b fp aa9d8e88 vs /course-admin/academic-progress 240,595 b fp f32306f0; Stop 1 served at both /student-support/advice-and-help/stop-1 and /support-and-wellbeing/stop-1). The split shows up as competing inbound links: the old Stop 1 path is linked 1,648× estate-wide vs 224× on the new path; the old visa advanced-standing path 613× vs 303× new. The hub actually exposes 11 substantive top-level roots, not the "four" the corpus asserts (structural-findings.md Finding 23) — the lean exemplar held up to standardise faculties against is itself the most internally-inconsistent IA in the estate. Do: Pick one canonical root per concept (the analytics-winning URL — data suggests /support-and-wellbeing and /your-course are the newer targets), 301-redirect all 120 legacy leaves, add rel=canonical, and update the high-fan-in links (the 1,648 to old Stop 1, 613 to old visa). Add a redirect-coverage check so no path has live inbound on both trees. This must land FIRST — faculties are being told to link into a hub whose own URLs are unstable. Acceptance test: re-run the dual-path leaf query; target 0 leaves under 2+ roots (currently 120).

1.2 One URL convention for the current-students entry — top-level /students [HIGH · medium]

A "first segment containing student" query confirms five conventions at different depths: depth-0 /students (eng 222, law 201, arts 92, science 67, fbe 43), depth-0 /current-students (msd 993, ffam 47), and depth-2+ buried inside the prospective /study funnel (mdhs /study/current-students 17, medicine /study/current-student-resources 16, biomed /study/current-student-information 42, dental /study/student-profiles 5; education buries 107 under /study/graduate-research/current-students). A student's current-students home is sometimes a top-level nav item and sometimes 2+ clicks deep inside prospective content. Do: Mandate top-level /students (the modal pattern, 5 faculties) and 301-redirect /current-students, /study/current-students, /study/current-student-information/-resources, /study/student-profiles to it. Pull the MDHS/medicine/biomed/dental student sections OUT of /study (prospective) up to /students (enrolled). Redirect + nav-config task, not a content rebuild.

1.3 No two faculties share a top-level taxonomy — there is no common IA to standardise into [HIGH · large]

First-path-segment analysis of all 12 faculty index.json files: the dominant top-level section differs in every domain and is almost never the student section — mdhs 89% /research (5,387/6,072), medicine 63% /school-structure (2,015/3,213), ffam 51% /about-us, law 49% /centres, dental 47% /engage, science 43% /engage, msd 42% /current-students, biomed 30% /departments, fbe 23% /newsroom, eng 21% /mric, education 20% /faces-of-education, arts 13% /school-of-social-and-political-sciences. Section count ranges 9 (science, dental) → 54 (arts). This is the parent-architecture root cause of the five-URL-convention problem. Do: Do not try to unify parent faculty taxonomies (they serve research/news/industry, not students). Mandate a thin, consistent student overlay every faculty exposes identically regardless of parent IA: a fixed /students path, a fixed set of section labels, and a fixed "what's here vs what's on students.unimelb" block. The only lever that touches all 12 architectures without a rebuild — and it depends on the hub IA being stable first (1.1).

1.4 Research-led faculties demote the student entry below research [MEDIUM · large]

The three medical-cluster domains the corpus's 9-faculty cut omitted are the most extreme: mdhs 89% /research with students at /study/current-students (17 pp); medicine 63% /school-structure with students at /study/current-student-resources (16 pp); biomed 30% /departments + large /sbs-research-groups tail with students at /study/current-student-information (42 pp). These three also contribute almost nothing to the hub (medicine 21, mdhs 175, biomed 0 contextual links into students.unimelb) — confirming the "research paradox" (biggest research estates carry least student support) at the IA level. Do: For mdhs, medicine, biomed, the overlay mandate must promote the student entry to a stable top-level /students node OUT of the research/study hierarchy, plus a persistent global-nav "Current students" link. Treat these three as the priority pilot — their fragmentation cost is highest.

1.5 Navigation depth has no estate standard — content buried 9–11 levels deep [MEDIUM · medium]

URL-depth histogram over the 12 faculty index.json files: mean path depth 3.12 (mdhs) → 5.36 (msd) and 5.06 (medicine), max 9–11 (eng 11, law 10, biomed 10, msd 9, medicine 9). The deep tail is concentrated in marketing-led estates: MSD 397 pages at depth 6 and 888 at depth 7–8; medicine 560 at depth 6 and 716 at depth 7–8; law 507 at depth 6. nav-depth.csv corroborates (msd 65% of pages ≥5 deep, medicine 59%, study.unimelb 58%). Do: Add a depth target (2–3 levels for task content, hard cap ~5) to the overlay template; prioritise re-nesting MSD's /current-students tree (993 pp) and Medicine's /school-structure (2,015 pp). Pair with a breadcrumb requirement so deep pages stay orientable.

2.3 Fix the ?a= artifact manufacturing phantom 404s on live pages [HIGH · quick-win]

The root cause behind the estate's apparent broken-link mass. 32 paths return "Page not found" only when a trailing ?a= is appended (medicine /school-structure?a=, msd /maker-spaces?a=, biomed /departments?a=) — but the canonical paths are live (medicine /school-structure is a working 187KB "Departments at Melbourne Medical School"). Because URL normalisation strips the query, the ?a= 404 twin merges with the canonical path and tags it dead. Of 4,316 contextual links that appear to hit 404s, 3,359 (78%) actually point at 30 destinations whose canonical URL is live and 404 only via ?a= — including the 1,143 in-content "Our Departments" links on Medicine. hubs.csv lists medicine/school-structure?a= as the #2 estate "hub" (1,146) purely from this artifact. Do: Find the template/component emitting the trailing ?a= (an unresolved anchor/analytics token) and remove it, or add a server rule stripping an empty a param before routing. Single fix clears ~3,359 phantom-broken links and collapses ~32 phantom-404 "hubs" back into live pages. No page restoration needed.

2.4 Sweep the ~414 genuinely-dead destinations (957 links) — after the ?a= fix [MEDIUM · medium]

After separating the ?a= artifact, the residual genuinely-dead surface is 957 contextual links → 419 destinations (only 5 synthetic /404 endpoints; ~414 real gaps, thin long tail — top is msd/404 itself at 67, then ≤8 each). Corpus-wide 493 error-titled pages (title scan of 27,302 pages; dead-pages.csv lists 499), concentrated in the MDHS cluster (biomedical 153, msd 74, fbe 72, mdhs 53, medicine 51). A few are live failures the CMS still links to: law mls-staff-listing-records/.../judith-grbich (404), .../dudi-rulliadi (502), mdhs diversity-and-inclusion/.../health-and-wellbeing (502), research commercialisation/researchers/our-entrepreneurial-ecosystem (502). Do: Run the ?a= fix first; then a one-off sweep over the ~414 dead destinations — restore or 301 each, fix the referring template. Prioritise by faculty CMS (long tail, not a top-20). Stand up a recurring broken-link report keyed on title='Page not found', reported against canonical URLs so ?a= twins don't re-inflate it.

2.5 Stop treating search-result URLs as canonical destinations [HIGH · large]

Add rel=canonical on results pages pointing at a clean landing page; strip tracking/filter params from outbound template links; governance rule: in-content links must target a stable content URL, never a paginated/filtered search result.

Round-2 dimension (round 1 omitted both). Mobile setup is fine — viewport meta is present on 600/600 sampled pages; the problem is payload weight and render cost, which hits students on phones/limited data hardest.

Multi-MB pages from base64-inlined hero images crush mobile/limited-data students [HIGH · medium]

Evidence: First-hand HTML analysis of all 154 pages >500KB in /crawl found ~9 pages shipping >100KB of base64-encoded images embedded directly in the HTML document (not as cacheable image files): finearts-music.unimelb.edu.au/about-us/news/meet-nicole-goode,-honours-visual-art-graduate = 2,292KB total, 2,071KB is a SINGLE inlined photo; mdhs.unimelb.edu.au/diversity-and-inclusion/initiatives/dei-conference = 2,704KB (2,501KB base64); msd.unimelb.edu.au/thus/news-events/urban-density-and-access-to-social-services = 1,779KB (1,617KB); mdhs .../mdhs-diversity-and-inclusion-grants = 1,220KB (1,054KB); law.unimelb.edu.au/news/MLS/from-education-to-precision-use = 1,145KB (976KB); biomedicalsciences .../cmr/about-us/contact-us = 734KB (570KB). page-weight.csv confirms these as the per-domain max outliers (finearts max 2,292KB, medicine 2,516KB, msd 1,779KB). Base64 inlining defeats browser caching, lazy-loading, responsive srcset, and CDN image optimization, and inflates the HTML ~33% (encoding overhead) — every byte is re-downloaded on every visit. On a 3G/limited-data plan a 2MB news article is a ~10–20s wait and a real data cost.

Do: Add a CMS publish-time guard (and a crawl-based linter over freshness-signals tooling) that blocks/flags any inline data:image payload over ~10KB; convert offending images to served files with width-appropriate srcset, loading="lazy", and modern formats (WebP/AVIF). Run a one-off sweep to de-inline the ~9 known >100KB offenders. Set a hard page-weight budget (e.g. 500KB HTML) enforced in CI so editors cannot paste large base64 blobs from Word/design tools again.

Cookiebot external-link-arrow icon duplicated ~1,925× per page inflates staff pages to 5MB [HIGH · quick-win]

These are the absolute max outliers in page-weight.csv (law max 5,056KB, mdhs max 4,730KB). gzip masks but does not fix it (the law page still parses 5MB of DOM = 1,938 nodes the phone must build).
