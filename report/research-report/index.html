Executive summary

One journey, twenty-plus systems

The current-student journey is not served by a single estate but by 20+ public systems with no shared information architecture or vocabulary — and the link graph shows the sprawl is deeper still: 415 distinct *.unimelb.edu.au hosts appear in the contextual graph alone, plus ~18 single-purpose form microsites and an authenticated core (my.unimelb, Canvas) that sits behind login.

Across the faculty domains, current students are only a 4–7% tenant of estates built for reputation, research and recruitment. No two faculties share a top-level taxonomy, and the two ends of the lifecycle are barely wired: after stripping navigation chrome, only ~97 contextual links reach the alumni estate and only ~100 reach central Careers — the estate's weakest seams.

Meanwhile the canonical hub, students.unimelb.edu.au (833 pages, not the "74" previously assumed), is itself mid-migration — dual-serving ~120 leaf pages under two parallel URL trees — while its single biggest dependency, the Handbook, sits on a different platform at a visibly lower web standard.

The opportunity is therefore overwhelmingly structural and governance-led, not visual: a thin standardised student overlay on the platform the University already runs everywhere, a subdomain/forms registry to stop re-fragmentation, completion of the in-flight hub migration, and a set of template and redirect fixes that each clear thousands of links at once.

Method

From a 5 GB crawl to a content-connection map

The analysis is grounded in data, not impressions. Every reachable public page was crawled with its full HTML and outbound links, then reduced to the connections that carry meaning.

URLs crawled → 21,250 unique content pages with full HTML + link data
862k
raw outbound links captured at path level (href, anchor text, host)
598
global-nav / footer "chrome" link patterns detected and stripped
262k
genuinely contextual links remaining — the real content connections

The decisive step is chrome-stripping: a link that appears on more than 60% of a domain's pages is global navigation, not a content connection. Removing it is what separates "every page links to Alumni in the header" from "almost no page points a student to Alumni on purpose." Findings were then produced by two multi-agent workflows — analyse → adversarially verify → synthesise → critique — with every claim re-checked against the source data.

Key findings

What the path-level data revealed

Several headline claims changed once the analysis could see link paths, not just hostnames. Findings marked corrected overturn or sharpen an earlier conclusion.

Corrected

The alumni handoff is nav-only, not "zero links"

Faculty pages link /alumni on nearly every page — but that is global navigation. After stripping chrome, only ~97 contextual links reach the alumni estate, and graduation-day pages link Facebook and Instagram, not the alumni relationship.

~97 contextual links estate-wide · 0 from any graduation/completion page
Corrected

The "broken links" are mostly a ?a= artifact

Of 4,316 links that appeared to hit 404s, 3,359 point at live pages that only error when a stray ?a= query string is appended (e.g. Medicine's "Our Departments", linked 1,143×). One template fix clears them; only ~414 pages are genuinely dead.

3,359 of 4,316 "broken" links are phantom · ~414 truly dead
Corrected

The hub is mid-migration and unstable

The site faculties are told to standardise against dual-serves ~120 leaf pages under two parallel URL trees (/your-course+/course-admin, /student-support+/support-and-wellbeing). Standardisation can't start until this is resolved.

120 dual-served leaves · old Stop 1 path still linked 1,648×
Risk

8,069 deep-links aimed at paths being abandoned

MSD alone sends 8,069 contextual links into the hub — 99% onto the legacy URL trees the migration is retiring. Without permanent redirects, finishing the migration breaks thousands of links at once.

8,069 links · ~99% on legacy paths · next faculty: 275
Structure

Current students are a 4–7% tenant

Faculty domains are built for reputation, research and recruitment. The dominant section is research (MDHS 89%), schools, or news — never the student section. There is no common faculty IA to standardise into.

Students = 4–7% of each faculty estate · no shared top-level taxonomy
Performance

Pages are needlessly heavy on mobile

Hero images base64-inlined into HTML produce 2–2.7 MB pages; a Cookiebot icon duplicated ~1,925× inflates staff pages to 5 MB; the student course template ships ~327 KB before any external file loads.

study.unimelb median 304 KB / max 1.1 MB · viewport setup itself is fine
High-stakes journey

Fees are a reverse-funnel

355 fee pages live on the prospective site versus 17 for enrolled students, so current students are bounced back to marketing for fee answers — and the census-dates page carries a canonical=/page-not-found.

355 fee pages on study.unimelb · 17 on students.unimelb
High-stakes journey

Visa compliance is forked across three trees

The CoE / visa / OSHC chain exists as three live trees — including a /sandbox/2026-uplifts draft that is live and indexed — and 11 faculties link the deprecated one. The two highest-risk pages link to zero action forms.

3 live trees · 11 faculties link the wrong one · 0 action links

Confidence

What this report does — and doesn't — establish

The analysis is grounded in the public estate. Five boundaries a reader must carry forward before any item ships.

Scope & what is not yet verified

  1. The authenticated core is uncrawlable. my.unimelb (SIS), Canvas and the ~18 form microsites sit behind login. Recommendations to "wire X to my.unimelb / a form" are inferred from the public side — confirm the logged-in flow before scoping.
  2. 404 / redirect claims are URL-string inference, not live HTTP. No HTTP request was issued; the ?a= artifact and dead-link counts need HEAD-probe confirmation of real status codes before remediation.
  3. Findings are structural-evidence-only, not CX-validated. "Add a bridge / CTA" items rest on link-graph absence, not observed student failure — validate the costliest seams with real users.
  4. No analytics or traffic data. Link-volume is the importance proxy throughout; it is not demand. Internal tracking params suggest this data exists — requesting it is the single highest-value next step, so priority can follow the pages students actually hit.
  5. Two evidence notes. Near-duplicate claims are now reproducible (near_dup.py); the Handbook "14% orphaned" figure is largely a bot-block artifact pending a JS-enabled re-crawl; and students.unimelb is 833 pages crawled / 679 with substantive content (both correct, different denominators).
